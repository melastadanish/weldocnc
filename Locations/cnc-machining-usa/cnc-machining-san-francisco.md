## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for San Francisco and Bay Area Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to San Francisco?**
Total lead time to San Francisco is 6–12 business days — 1–5 business days manufacturing in Dongguan plus 5–7 business days shipping via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. All shipments are DDP — import duties and US customs clearance are handled and included in the quoted price. Parts arrive at your San Francisco or Bay Area address with no additional import charges.

**Q2: What tolerance can Weldo CNC hold on parts for Bay Area robotics and tech customers?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard tolerance for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features. Bay Area robotics and consumer electronics prototyping typically specifies tolerances in the ±0.005–0.020 mm range; Weldo CNC covers this fully, with the dimensional report shipped with every tight-tolerance order.

**Q3: How does Weldo CNC compare to Bay Area machine shops?**
Bay Area shops handle urgent 24–48 hour jobs and locally auditable programmes. For everything else — tight tolerances, full documentation, specialist materials, or quantities above 10 parts — Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice. Bay Area machining rates reflect Bay Area real estate and labour costs. A 20-part pre-production run of PEEK components with CMM documentation from a Bay Area shop will cost significantly more than the same order delivered DDP from Dongguan in 6–12 days. Send the drawing and compare quotes directly.

**Q4: Does Weldo CNC include DFM feedback with every quote for SF engineers?**
Yes — design for manufacturability review is included with every quote, not billed separately. If the geometry has a feature that will cause problems — thin walls below 0.5 mm, thread depth issues, surface finish conflicts with tolerance — the quote response identifies it. Bay Area engineers iterating fast on robotics or consumer electronics hardware get the feedback on the first quote, not after the first batch comes back with a problem.

**Q5: Do you supply SF Bay Area medtech and life sciences companies?**
Yes — surgical instrument prototypes, diagnostic device housings, and laboratory hardware in 316L stainless steel, titanium Grade 5, and PEEK are within standard capability. Full material traceability and CMM dimensional reports are standard on every order. For South San Francisco or Mission Bay biomedical programmes requiring specific documentation, confirm your requirements at quoting.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces due to fixed setup cost, but there is no floor on order size. Bay Area startups ordering a single prototype CNC part pay only unit cost and DDP shipping — no MOQ surcharge.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is the preferred format for 3D models. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Upload directly — response within 24 hours.

**Q8: How does pricing compare to Bay Area machine shops for prototype and pre-production quantities?**
For a single urgent part, a local Bay Area shop may be competitive and faster. From 10 parts upward — especially with full CMM documentation — Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower on unit cost. Bay Area shops carry some of the highest overhead in US manufacturing; that cost appears in every quote. For a 50-part pre-production run of aluminium robotics components with full documentation, the difference is meaningful. Upload the STEP file and compare directly.

**Q9: Can Weldo CNC handle Apple and Google adjacent consumer electronics prototyping work?**
Yes — precision aluminium and PEEK components for consumer electronics product development programmes are within standard capability. Surface finishes from Ra 0.8 µm mirror polish to Type III hard anodize. DFM feedback included with every quote. No minimum order. The supply chain adjacent to major Bay Area consumer electronics companies routinely specifies tight tolerances and specific surface finish requirements — Weldo CNC covers both at pre-production quantities.

**Q10: Can I visit the Weldo CNC facility?**
The facility is in Dongguan, China. Visits from engineers wanting to audit the operation are welcome — arrange with advance notice. For most Bay Area customers, the CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and ISO 9001:2015 certification provide sufficient confidence without travel. Video walkthrough available on request.
