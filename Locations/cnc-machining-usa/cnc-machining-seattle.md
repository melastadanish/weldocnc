**Heading:** CNC Machining for Seattle Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Seattle?**
Total lead time to Seattle is 6–12 business days — 1–5 business days manufacturing in Dongguan plus 5–7 business days shipping via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. Seattle-Tacoma International is a major Pacific Rim freight hub, which means reliable inbound customs clearance from China. All shipments are DDP — import duties and US customs clearance handled and included in the quoted price. Parts arrive with no additional import charges.

**Q2: What tolerance can Weldo CNC hold on aerospace and precision parts for Seattle customers?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard tolerance for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features. Seattle aerospace supply chain work commonly specifies tolerances in the ±0.005–0.015 mm range on aerostructure components; Weldo CNC covers this fully, with every critical dimension reported against your drawing and shipped with the parts.

**Q3: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Seattle aerospace machine shops?**
Local Seattle aerospace shops — particularly those in Renton and Kent serving the Boeing supply chain — are experienced, often AS9100D certified, and well suited to programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier. These shops run at high utilisation during production rate increases, with queue times of 4–8 weeks on complex work. Weldo CNC is the stronger choice for secondary supply chain sourcing, pre-production quantities, or any job where the 6–12 day total delivery time is acceptable and documentation requirements are met by ISO 9001:2015 with CMM reporting. AS9100D certification is not held — confirm if your programme requires it.

**Q4: Does Weldo CNC supply parts for Amazon robotics and Microsoft hardware programmes?**
Yes — actuator components, gripper bodies, conveyor mechanism hardware, and precision aluminium enclosures for technology hardware programmes are within standard capability. DFM review is included with every quote. No minimum order. Prototype iterations are quoted within 24 hours of file upload.

**Q5: Can Weldo CNC supply marine-grade stainless steel components for Seattle maritime customers?**
Yes — marine hardware in 316L stainless steel with passivation is within standard capability. Material certificates with heat numbers are included with every order — essential for marine-grade material traceability. Short to medium production runs of 10–100 pieces are the typical order size for Seattle maritime customers; Weldo CNC handles this range without MOQ pressure.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces due to fixed setup cost, but there is no floor on order size. Seattle engineers ordering a single prototype part pay only unit cost and DDP shipping.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is the preferred format for 3D models. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For precision features — particularly on aerospace components with tight callouts — always include a 2D drawing with tolerance annotations alongside the STEP file. Upload on the quote page — response within 24 hours.

**Q8: How does pricing compare to Seattle aerospace precision shops?**
For a single urgent part, a local Seattle shop is the right answer. For pre-production quantities — 20 to 200 parts — with CMM documentation, material certificates, and finish records, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower on unit cost. Seattle aerospace shops carry programme overhead, AS9100D certification costs, and regional labour rates in their pricing. Secondary supply chain sourcing at Weldo CNC's documentation standard is the practical choice when local capacity is constrained. Upload the STEP file and compare.

**Q9: Does Weldo CNC hold AS9100D certification?**
No — Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification. AS9100D is not currently certified. For Boeing supply chain programmes that mandate AS9100D certification at the machining source, a locally certified shop is the required supplier. For programmes where ISO 9001:2015 with CMM reporting and material traceability satisfies the quality requirement, Weldo CNC covers the documentation standard in full. Confirm your programme's certification requirement before placing an order.

**Q10: Can I visit the Weldo CNC facility?**
The facility is in Dongguan, China. Seattle is well-connected to Dongguan via Seattle-Tacoma International — visits from engineers wanting to audit the operation are welcome with advance notice. For most Seattle customers, the CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and ISO 9001:2015 certification provide sufficient confidence without travel. Video walkthrough available on request.
