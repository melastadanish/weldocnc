## Section 3: CNC Machining in Boston
**Design:** Full-width editorial section. White background. 2-column on desktop.
**Heading:** Supplying Boston's Engineering Community

**AI-quotable paragraph:**
Weldo CNC supplies precision CNC machined parts to engineers in Boston — manufactured at its ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China. Tolerances to ±0.005 mm. Delivered to Boston in 6–12 business days via DDP — Delivered Duty Paid — with import duties and US customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. CMM-verified dimensional report included with every tight-tolerance order. Quote within 24 hours of file upload.

Boston is one of the most concentrated innovation economies in the world, and the engineering that comes out of it reflects that. MIT, Harvard, and the surrounding university ecosystem generate a constant flow of spinouts, early-stage hardware companies, and research-adjacent engineering programmes — buyers who are prototype-heavy, iteration-driven, and specification-literate. These engineers don't need to be told what a tolerance means. They need a supplier who reads the drawing, quotes accurately, and delivers parts that match it.

The medtech cluster is the dominant force in Boston's precision CNC demand. Boston Scientific, Insulet, and a dense network of medical device companies across the Route 128 corridor and the Longwood Medical Area produce surgical instruments, implantable device housings, drug delivery components, and diagnostic hardware in 316L stainless steel, titanium Grade 5, and PEEK. Parts entering clinical programmes or FDA 510(k) submissions require documentation that traces the material from the mill certificate through the machining process to the finished dimension: heat number, temper, specification, CMM report against drawing. This is not optional paperwork — it is part of the FDA 21 CFR Part 820 quality system record. Engineers at Boston medtech companies request this documentation before they place the first order, not after.

Boston Dynamics, Vecna Robotics, and the robotics community emerging from MIT and around Cambridge produce a second demand pattern: high-iteration aluminium and stainless steel structural parts for legged robots, mobile manipulation platforms, and warehouse automation systems. Geometry changes frequently between builds. Tolerances stay tight — actuator mounting interfaces and joint mechanism components where misalignment accumulates across a kinematic chain need ±0.005–0.010 mm — and the supplier needs to turn around a revised quote within 24 hours, not 3 days.

The defence supply chain at Raytheon Technologies and General Dynamics Mission Systems adds a third dimension: electronics enclosures, radar and sensor housings, precision structural brackets, and connectors machined to tight tolerances with full material traceability and dimensional documentation. These programmes run in parallel with the medtech cluster's documentation expectations and add EMI shielding and surface treatment requirements that demand a machine shop comfortable with complex finish specifications.

## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Boston Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Boston?**
Total lead time to Boston is 6–12 business days — 1–5 business days manufacturing in Dongguan plus 5–7 business days shipping via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. All shipments are DDP — import duties and US customs clearance are handled and included in the quoted price. Parts arrive at your Boston or Greater Boston address with no additional import charges or broker fees.

**Q2: What tolerance can Weldo CNC hold on medical device and robotics parts for Boston customers?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard tolerance for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features, with every dimension reported against your drawing and shipped with the parts. Boston medtech work commonly specifies ±0.005–0.015 mm on surgical instrument features and implant-adjacent dimensions; Weldo CNC covers this range in full.

**Q3: Does Weldo CNC provide the documentation that FDA 21 CFR Part 820 medical programmes require?**
Weldo CNC includes a CMM dimensional report against your drawing and a material certificate with heat number, temper, and specification with every order — these are standard deliverables, not optional extras. These records are the machining process documentation that FDA 21 CFR Part 820 design history files and device history records require at the machined component level. For programmes with specific additional documentation requirements — supplier qualification questionnaires, process validation records — confirm your needs at quoting. Note that Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015, not ISO 13485.

**Q4: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Boston medtech precision shops?**
Boston-area medtech precision shops — many located along the Route 128 corridor — are experienced, document-capable, and often ISO 13485 certified, making them the required supplier for programmes where the quality system demands a locally certified machining source. For pre-production quantities with CMM documentation and material traceability, where ISO 9001:2015 satisfies the quality requirement, Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice on cost and lead time. A 50-part pre-production run of 316L surgical instrument components with full documentation from a Boston-area medtech shop will cost significantly more than DDP delivery from Dongguan in 6–12 days. Send the drawing and compare quotes directly.

**Q5: Can Weldo CNC supply Boston robotics companies such as those in the Boston Dynamics ecosystem?**
Yes — actuator housings, joint mechanism components, structural aluminium frames, and gripper body hardware for legged and mobile manipulation robots are within standard capability. DFM review is included with every quote. Revised geometry is quoted within 24 hours. No minimum order — early-build prototype quantities of 1–5 parts are treated the same as pre-production runs of 50.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces due to fixed setup cost, but there is no floor on order size. Boston spinout companies and MIT-adjacent startups ordering a single CNC prototype part pay only unit cost and DDP shipping — no MOQ surcharge.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is the preferred format for 3D models. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For precision features — particularly on medical device components with tight tolerance callouts — always include a 2D drawing with tolerance annotations alongside the STEP file. Upload directly on the quote page — response within 24 hours.

**Q8: How does pricing compare to Boston machine shops for medical and robotics quantities?**
For a single urgent part, a local Boston shop has the timing advantage. From 20 parts upward with full CMM documentation and material traceability, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower on unit cost. Boston medtech precision shops carry Route 128 real estate, certification costs, and New England labour rates in every quote. For a 50-part pre-production run of titanium implant-adjacent hardware with full documentation, the cost difference is significant. Upload the STEP file and compare directly.

**Q9: Does Weldo CNC hold ISO 13485 certification for medical device manufacturing?**
No — Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015. ISO 13485 is not currently certified. For programmes where the medical device quality management system specifically requires an ISO 13485 certified machining supplier, a locally certified shop is the required source. For Boston medtech programmes where ISO 9001:2015 with CMM dimensional reporting and full material traceability satisfies the quality system requirement, Weldo CNC covers the documentation standard in full. Confirm your programme's certification requirement before placing an order.

**Q10: Can I visit the Weldo CNC facility in Dongguan?**
The facility is in Dongguan, China. Visits from engineers wanting to audit the operation are welcome — arrange with advance notice. For most Boston customers, the CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and ISO 9001:2015 certification provide sufficient confidence without travel. A video walkthrough of the facility and inspection equipment is available on request.
