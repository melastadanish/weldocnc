## Section 3: CNC Machining in Edinburgh
**Design:** Full-width editorial section. White background. 2-column on desktop.
**Heading:** Supplying Edinburgh's Engineering Community

Edinburgh's engineering demand is shaped by two industries that place some of the most demanding requirements on precision CNC machined components: energy — particularly oil and gas and offshore renewables — and defence. Both sectors expect material traceability, dimensional verification, and documentation as standard. Both operate in environments where component failure is not a cost issue but a safety issue.

The North Sea oil and gas industry — with its onshore engineering and procurement operations concentrated in Aberdeen, but with significant activity across the wider Scottish central belt including Edinburgh — generates requirements for downhole tooling, valve bodies, manifold assemblies, choke components, and subsea hardware. Material specifications in this sector are demanding: duplex stainless (2205, 2507), super-duplex, Inconel 625 and 718, and 17-4PH stainless are common. Pressure ratings and material traceability documentation — material test reports, NACE MR0175 compliance confirmation, PMI (Positive Material Identification) records — are typically required. Under DDP, Weldo CNC provides material certificates with heat number, temper, and specification for every order; PMI testing can be specified at quoting.

The offshore wind sector — with major development activity in the Firth of Forth, Moray Firth, and broader Scottish waters — is creating a growing secondary stream of machined component demand: structural fasteners, bearing housings, hydraulic fittings, and precision parts for marine and subsea systems. The material and documentation requirements overlap significantly with oil and gas.

Scotland's defence sector — with BAE Systems at Scotstoun (naval vessels), Babcock at Rosyth (naval refit and support), and QinetiQ and Leonardo operations across central Scotland — generates demand for precision aluminium, stainless, and specialist alloy components for naval, land systems, and electronic warfare programmes. The Edinburgh and central Scotland defence supply chain is well-developed, with engineers who understand what a proper dimensional report looks like and will read it.

## Section 10: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Edinburgh Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Edinburgh?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. Scottish Highlands and Islands: add 1–2 days. No import charges on arrival.

**Q2: Can you machine duplex stainless, Inconel, and other energy-sector alloys?**
Yes — duplex 2205, super-duplex 2507, Inconel 625 and 718, 17-4PH stainless, and a broad range of other high-performance alloys are within our standard capability. Material certificates with heat number and full specification ship with every order. PMI testing can be specified at quoting for programmes that require it.

**Q3: Can you provide the documentation required for oil and gas programmes?**
Material certificates (mill cert, heat number, temper, specification) and CMM dimensional reports are standard on every order. For NACE MR0175 compliance confirmation, specific material test reports, or other energy-sector documentation requirements, specify at quoting and we will confirm what can be provided.

**Q4: How do you compare to local Edinburgh and Aberdeen precision shops?**
Local shops are well suited to urgent work and programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier. For tight-tolerance work in specialist alloys with full documentation, quantities above 10 parts, or complex 5-axis geometry, Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice on cost and lead time. Send us the drawing and we'll quote.

**Q5: What if there's a quality issue?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part doesn't conform and the report confirms it, we remake or credit — without dispute.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities.
