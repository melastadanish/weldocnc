Marseille's engineering sector is shaped by two overriding forces: the Mediterranean port and the energy industry. Both create sustained demand for precision machined components that must withstand harsh environments — saltwater exposure, high pressure, elevated temperature, and chemical contact.

Naval engineering and maritime equipment drive the primary CNC machining requirement. Naval Group, France's naval defence prime, operates its principal submarine and frigate production out of Toulon — a 60-kilometre drive from Marseille — and its procurement and engineering activity extends across the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur region. Naval propulsion components, hull fittings, valve bodies, underwater hardware, and pressure vessel components are machined in stainless steel, brass, bronze, and specialist marine-grade alloys. Surface protection is critical: passivation, electropolishing, and nickel plating are common finish requirements on stainless components that will see seawater exposure.

Port and logistics equipment adds a second requirement. Marseille's role as France's largest port — the Port of Marseille-Fos handles 80+ million tonnes of cargo annually — means a large base of port equipment operators and maintenance engineers who specify machined parts for material handling equipment, crane components, dock fittings, and hydraulic systems. These are typically medium-complexity parts in steel and stainless steel, specified at standard tolerances with the durability expectation of port-grade equipment.

Energy is the third pillar. TotalEnergies maintains significant Provence operations. Chemical refinery equipment — flanges, pump bodies, impellers, valve internals — is machined in specialist stainless grades (316L, 317L), duplex stainless, and sometimes titanium where process chemistry demands it. Tolerance requirements are more moderate than aerospace work, but material traceability and corrosion resistance documentation are non-negotiable in refinery procurement.

Local Marseille-area machine shops handle much of the standard port and maintenance work. Tight-tolerance naval and energy components, specialist materials, and larger batches requiring full documentation tend to stretch local capacity. Weldo CNC operates 100+ CNC machines in Dongguan with 1–5 business day manufacturing lead times. For Marseille engineers sourcing pre-production runs or documentation-heavy parts outside a long-term local programme, that capacity is available without queue.

## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Marseille Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Marseille?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority — after manufacturing. Total lead time is 4–10 business days combining 1–5 days manufacturing with 3–5 days shipping. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. Parts arrive in Marseille with no import charges on your end.

**Q2: Does Weldo CNC machine marine-grade stainless steel for Marseille customers?**
Yes — stainless steel 316L and 316 are confirmed materials, and these are the standard marine-grade austenitic grades for saltwater-exposed components. Also confirmed: 317L for higher chloride resistance, 17-4PH for higher-strength applications, and duplex stainless where strength and corrosion resistance are both required. Material certificate with heat number, temper, and specification ships with every stainless steel order. Passivation and electropolishing are available to confirm and document the corrosion-resistant surface condition.

**Q3: What surface finishes are available for naval and maritime components?**
Passivation — electrochemical removal of free iron from stainless steel surfaces — is confirmed and ships with a process record. Electropolishing — which removes the surface layer and produces a smooth, low-roughness finish that resists bacterial growth and corrosion — is also confirmed. Nickel plating for marine hardware, bead blasting for uniform matte texture, and hard anodising for aluminium components are all available. Every finish ships with a process record documenting the treatment. Confirm your finish specification at quoting.

**Q4: What import duties apply to CNC machined parts shipped to Marseille?**
CNC machined parts under CN 8466.93 attract EU import duty of 2.7–3.7%. TVA at 20% applies on import and is reclaimable if your company is VAT-registered in France. Weldo CNC ships DDP — Delivered Duty Paid under Incoterms 2020 — duties and customs clearance included in the quoted price. Marseille is France's largest port — DHL and FedEx handle import customs clearance through the established commercial freight system, not the port cargo system. No arrival charges on your end.

**Q5: Can Weldo CNC supply valve bodies and pump components for the energy sector in Marseille?**
Yes — industrial machinery is a confirmed industry vertical. Valve bodies, pump impellers, flanges, and hydraulic components in 316L stainless, duplex stainless, and specialist alloys are within standard CNC milling and turning capability. CMM-verified inspection report and material certificate ship with every order. For refinery and chemical processing applications, confirm your material specification and surface finish requirement at quoting — corrosion-resistant finishes and documented traceability are standard.

**Q6: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Marseille CNC shops?**
Local Marseille shops are the right answer for urgent 24–48 hour requirements and for naval programmes requiring locally auditable suppliers. For tight-tolerance work — ±0.005 mm — specialist stainless grades, marine-grade surface finishes with documentation, or batches of 10–500 parts where local capacity is committed to long-term clients, Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice. Manufacturing lead time of 1–5 days versus 2–6 weeks at local shops is the key differentiator for pre-production and development sourcing.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for quoting?**
STEP is the preferred format for 3D geometry. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For tight-tolerance features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings using M-thread series and ISO 2768 tolerance standards are the standard format.

**Q8: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC parts delivered to Marseille?**
No minimum order quantity. One part to full production quantities — both accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces due to fixed setup cost, but there is no floor. Marseille engineers sourcing prototype maritime fittings, first-article energy components, or pre-production batches of 5–50 parts are a typical use case.

**Q9: How does Weldo CNC verify quality before shipment to Marseille?**
Every tight-tolerance order ships with a CMM dimensional inspection report — coordinate measuring machine measurement of critical features against your drawing tolerances. Material certificate confirms heat number, temper, and specification. Finish process record documents the surface treatment. If a part doesn't conform and the report confirms it, Weldo CNC remakes or credits without dispute. The 97.48% annual pass rate reflects parts that clear inspection before shipping.

**Q10: Can Weldo CNC machine titanium for Marseille naval and energy customers?**
Titanium — TA1 (Grade 1), TA2 (Grade 2), and TC4/Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) — is a confirmed material across CNC milling, turning, and 5-axis machining. Titanium is used in naval and subsea applications where weight reduction combined with seawater corrosion resistance is required — Grade 2 (TA2) for general corrosion resistance, Grade 5 (TC4) where structural strength is also needed. Material certificate with heat number, temper, and alloy specification ships with every titanium order.
