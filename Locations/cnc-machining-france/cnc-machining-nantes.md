**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Nantes?**

**Q2: Does Weldo CNC supply aerospace parts for Airbus Atlantic programmes in Nantes?**
Yes — aerospace is a confirmed industry vertical with active customers. Aluminium 7075-T6, 6061-T6, and titanium TC4 machined parts with CMM-verified inspection reports and full material traceability are standard on every tight-tolerance order. Parts ship with material certificate (heat number, temper, specification), CMM inspection report, and finish process record. For Airbus Atlantic supply chain programmes requiring AS9100D-approved suppliers, confirm your documentation requirements at quoting — Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015. Many Nantes engineers use Weldo CNC for development quantities and first-article work.

**Q3: Can Weldo CNC supply marine and naval machined parts for Saint-Nazaire shipbuilding?**
Yes — marine and naval components are within confirmed capability. Valve bodies, shaft fittings, deck hardware, and hydraulic components in 316L stainless, brass, and bronze are standard CNC turning and milling work. Full material traceability and CMM-verified inspection are standard on every order. For Chantiers de l'Atlantique supply chain or bespoke vessel components, upload your drawing and confirm material and finish specification at quoting.

**Q4: What tolerances does Weldo CNC hold for Nantes aerospace and robotics work?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining — where the tool moves across all five axes simultaneously — ±0.003 mm is achievable on complex geometry. CMM inspection — coordinate measuring machine — verifies every critical-tolerance feature against your drawing. Nantes aerospace and robotics work commonly calls for ISO 2768 fine (f) as baseline, with tighter callouts on interface features and fastener hole patterns — both are within standard capability.

**Q5: What import duties apply to CNC machined parts shipped to Nantes?**
CNC machined parts under CN 8466.93 attract EU import duty of 2.7–3.7%. TVA at 20% applies on import and is reclaimable if your company is VAT-registered in France. Weldo CNC ships DDP — Delivered Duty Paid under Incoterms 2020 — duties and customs clearance included in the quoted price. No surprise charges on arrival in Nantes.

**Q6: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Nantes CNC machine shops?**
Local Nantes shops are the right answer for urgent 24–48 hour requirements and for programmes needing a locally auditable AS9100D-certified supplier in the Airbus Atlantic supply chain. For development and pre-production work — 1–50 part batches, geometry under revision, or first-article evaluation — Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice. Manufacturing lead time of 1–5 days versus 2–8 weeks at local shops committed to long-term Airbus and Chantiers de l'Atlantique supply is the most common decision factor.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for quoting?**
STEP is the preferred format for 3D geometry. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For tight-tolerance features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings using M-thread series and ISO 2768 tolerance standards are the standard format — NF norms referenced where supplied.

**Q8: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC parts delivered to Nantes?**
No minimum order quantity. One part to full production quantities — both accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces due to fixed setup cost, but there is no floor. Nantes engineers sourcing aerospace prototype brackets, naval bespoke fittings, or robotics actuator housings in batches of 1–20 parts are a typical use case.

**Q9: Can Weldo CNC machine PEEK and engineering plastics for the Pays de la Loire robotics cluster?**
PEEK — polyether ether ketone — is a confirmed material, CNC milled and turned. Also confirmed: POM (Delrin), nylon/PA, PTFE, and PC. For robotics applications, PEEK offers the stiffness and temperature resistance of engineering metals at lower weight — common in end-effector components, guide rails, and bearing housings. Surface finish on machined PEEK is controlled and documented — Ra 0.8 µm is achievable on critical surfaces.

**Q10: Does Weldo CNC provide surface finishing for Nantes aerospace and marine parts?**
50+ surface finish options are confirmed. For Nantes aerospace work: Type II and Type III anodising for aluminium, alodine chromate conversion, and bead blasting. For marine and naval stainless parts: passivation and electropolishing. Each finish ships with a process record documenting the treatment parameters. Confirm your finish specification — NF, ISO, or drawing-referenced — at quoting.
