## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Paris Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Paris?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority — after manufacturing. Total lead time is 4–10 business days combining 1–5 days manufacturing with 3–5 days shipping. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the price. Parts arrive in Paris with no import charges on your end and no customs administration required.

**Q2: What are the import duties and taxes for CNC parts shipped to Paris?**
CNC machined parts under commodity code CN 8466.93 attract an EU import duty of 2.7–3.7%. TVA (French VAT) at 20% applies on import and is reclaimable if your company is VAT-registered in France. Weldo CNC ships DDP — Delivered Duty Paid under Incoterms 2020 — which means duties and customs clearance are included in the quoted price. No surprise charges on arrival.

**Q3: Can Weldo CNC supply aerospace companies in Paris — Safran, Thales, and Dassault suppliers?**
Yes — aerospace is a confirmed industry vertical with active customers. Aluminium and titanium machined parts with CMM-verified dimensional reports and full material traceability are standard on every tight-tolerance order. Parts ship with material certificate (heat number, temper, specification), CMM inspection report, and finish process record. For programmes requiring AS9100D-certified suppliers, confirm your specific documentation requirements at quoting — Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 and documents against your drawing.

**Q4: What tolerances does Weldo CNC hold for Paris engineering work?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard tolerance for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm is achievable. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — inspection verifies every critical-tolerance feature against your drawing, and the report ships with the parts. Paris aerospace and defence work typically calls for ISO 2768 fine (f) or medium (m) class tolerances — both are covered at standard.

**Q5: How do you handle IP and drawing confidentiality for Paris customers?**
NDA is available before any file is transferred or drawing reviewed. Part data stays within the quoting and manufacturing team — it is not shared or reused. For aerospace and defence customers in Paris where IP sensitivity is high, NDA-first is the standard working practice, not an exception.

**Q6: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Paris CNC shops?**
Local Paris shops are the right answer for jobs needed in 24–48 hours, or programmes requiring a locally auditable AS9100D-certified supplier. For tight-tolerance work — ±0.005 mm and below — full documentation, specialist alloys (titanium, PEEK, 17-4PH), or quantities above 10 parts where Paris precision shop queue times and pricing become a constraint, Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice. Manufacturing lead time of 1–5 days versus a typical Paris shop queue of 3–6 weeks is the most common decision factor.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for quoting?**
STEP is the preferred format — it carries the 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For any tight-tolerance features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings using M-thread series and ISO 2768 tolerance standards are the standard format — NF norms can be referenced.

**Q8: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC machining delivered to Paris?**
No minimum order quantity. One piece to production quantities — both accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed, but there is no floor. Paris engineers sourcing prototype parts, first-articles, or pre-production samples of 5–50 parts are a typical use case.

**Q9: How does Weldo CNC verify quality before shipment to Paris?**
Every tight-tolerance order ships with a CMM dimensional inspection report — coordinate measuring machine measurement of critical features against your drawing tolerances. Material certificate confirms heat number, temper, and specification. If a part doesn't conform to the drawing and the report confirms it, Weldo CNC remakes or credits without dispute. The 97.48% annual pass rate reflects parts that clear inspection before shipping — not after.

**Q10: Can Weldo CNC handle titanium and specialist alloys for Paris aerospace customers?**
Titanium — TA1 (Grade 1), TA2 (Grade 2), and TC4/Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) — is a confirmed material across CNC milling, turning, and 5-axis machining. Ti-6Al-4V is the standard aerospace structural grade used in Paris-region aerospace programmes. 17-4PH stainless, MIC-6 tooling plate aluminium, and PEEK are also confirmed. Material certificate with heat number and temper is standard on all titanium and specialist alloy orders.
