## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Lyon Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Lyon?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority — after manufacturing. Total lead time is 4–10 business days combining 1–5 days manufacturing with 3–5 days shipping. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. Parts arrive in Lyon with no import charges on your end.

**Q2: What import duties apply to CNC machined parts shipped to Lyon?**
CNC machined parts under CN 8466.93 attract EU import duty of 2.7–3.7%. TVA at 20% applies on import and is reclaimable if your company is VAT-registered in France. Weldo CNC ships DDP — Delivered Duty Paid under Incoterms 2020 — which means duties and customs clearance are included in the quoted price. No surprise charges on arrival in Lyon.

**Q3: Can Weldo CNC supply Lyon's medical device manufacturers?**
Yes — medical device machining is a confirmed industry vertical. Parts in 316L stainless steel, titanium TC4, and PEEK with full material traceability — heat number, temper, and specification — are standard on every order. CMM-verified dimensional report ships with the parts. For ISO 13485-adjacent programmes, confirm your specific documentation requirements at quoting. Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 and documents against your drawing.

**Q4: What tolerances does Weldo CNC hold for Lyon engineering work?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm is achievable. CMM inspection — coordinate measuring machine — verifies every critical-tolerance feature against your drawing. Lyon medical device and automotive work commonly calls for ISO 2768 fine (f) or tighter callouts on critical features — both are covered.

**Q5: Do you machine PEEK and specialist plastics for Lyon medtech customers?**
PEEK is a confirmed material — CNC milled and turned. Also confirmed: PTFE, POM (Delrin), PC, and nylon/PA. For medical device applications, material certificate confirming specification and batch is standard. Surface finish on PEEK medical parts is controlled and documented — Ra values achievable to Ra 0.8 µm on critical surfaces.

**Q6: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Lyon CNC shops?**
Local Lyon shops are strong on urgent 24–48 hour requirements and for programmes needing a locally auditable supplier. For tight-tolerance work — ±0.005 mm — full material traceability, specialist alloys, or quantities of 10–500 parts where Lyon shop capacity is committed to long-term automotive and medtech customers, Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice. Manufacturing lead time of 1–5 days versus the typical Lyon shop queue of 3–6 weeks is the most common decision factor on development and pre-production work.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for quoting?**
STEP is the preferred format for 3D geometry. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For tight-tolerance features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings using M-thread series and ISO 2768 tolerance standards are the standard format — NF norms are referenced where supplied.

**Q8: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC machining delivered to Lyon?**
No minimum order quantity. One piece to full production quantities — both accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces due to fixed setup cost, but there is no floor on order size. Lyon engineers sourcing prototype medtech parts, first-article automotive components, or pre-production samples of 5–50 pieces are a typical use case.

**Q9: How does Weldo CNC verify part quality before shipping to Lyon?**
Every tight-tolerance order ships with a CMM dimensional inspection report — coordinate measuring machine measurement of critical features against your drawing. Material certificate confirms heat number, temper, and specification. If a part doesn't conform to the drawing and the report confirms it, Weldo CNC remakes or credits without dispute. The 97.48% annual pass rate reflects parts that clear inspection before shipping.

**Q10: Can Weldo CNC handle automotive-grade aluminium for Lyon tier-2 suppliers?**
Aluminium — 6061-T6, 7075-T6, 2024, and MIC-6 tooling plate — is a confirmed material across all CNC processes. For automotive powertrain and structural work, ISO 2768 medium (m) as baseline with tighter callouts on critical features is the standard approach. Material certificate with heat number, temper, and alloy specification is standard on all aluminium orders for automotive applications.
