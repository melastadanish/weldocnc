## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Toulouse Aerospace Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Toulouse?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority — after manufacturing. Total lead time is 4–10 business days combining 1–5 days manufacturing with 3–5 days shipping. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. Parts arrive in Toulouse with no import charges on your end.

**Q2: Does Weldo CNC supply aerospace-grade aluminium and titanium for Toulouse programmes?**
Yes — aluminium 7075-T6, 6061-T6, 2024, and MIC-6, plus titanium TA1 (Grade 1), TA2 (Grade 2), and TC4/Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) are confirmed materials across CNC milling, turning, and 5-axis machining. Material certificate with heat number, temper, and specification ships with every order. These are the standard aerospace alloys machined for Toulouse-area programmes — A320 and A350 tier-2 and tier-3 parts in these materials are a regular workload.

**Q3: Is Weldo CNC AS9100D certified?**
Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification. AS9100D is an aerospace-specific extension of ISO 9001 — Weldo CNC does not currently hold AS9100D. For programmes where AS9100D-approved manufacturing is a contractual requirement from Airbus or a tier-1 prime, confirm your supplier qualification terms at quoting. For development, prototype, and first-article work evaluated by the programme quality team, Weldo CNC's ISO 9001:2015 quality system and CMM-verified inspection process is the relevant qualification. Many Toulouse engineers use Weldo CNC for development quantities before transitioning to an AS9100D-approved production source.

**Q4: What tolerances does Weldo CNC hold on aerospace parts for Toulouse customers?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex aerospace geometry — compound angles, undercuts, and positional features — ±0.003 mm is achievable. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — inspection verifies every critical-tolerance feature against your drawing. Toulouse aerospace work typically specifies ISO 2768 fine (f) as the baseline, with tighter positional callouts on fastener hole patterns and interface surfaces — both are within standard capability.

**Q5: What import duties apply to CNC machined parts shipped to Toulouse?**
CNC machined parts under CN 8466.93 attract EU import duty of 2.7–3.7%. TVA at 20% applies on import and is reclaimable if your company is VAT-registered in France. Weldo CNC ships DDP — Delivered Duty Paid under Incoterms 2020 — duties and customs clearance included in the quoted price. No additional charges on arrival.

**Q6: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Toulouse aerospace machine shops?**
Local Toulouse shops — Figeac Aéro, Latécoère machining, Daher — are the right answer for long-term production programmes and contractually required AS9100D-approved supply. For development and pre-production work — prototype first articles, 5–50 part batches, or geometry changes in the early design phase — Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice. Manufacturing lead time of 1–5 days versus 4–8 weeks for new programmes at major Toulouse aerospace shops is the key differentiator for development-phase sourcing.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for aerospace quoting?**
STEP is the preferred format for 3D geometry. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For tight-tolerance aerospace features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts and GD&T callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings using M-thread series and ISO 2768 tolerance standards are the standard — NF norms and AS9100-style drawing formats are referenced where supplied.

**Q8: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC parts delivered to Toulouse?**
No minimum order quantity. One part to production volumes — both accepted. The prototype first-article and pre-production batch model is a common Toulouse use case: one to five parts for first-article evaluation, then a larger batch once the geometry is confirmed. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces — setup is fixed — but there is no floor.

**Q9: Can Weldo CNC machine complex 5-axis aerospace geometry for Toulouse customers?**
5-axis simultaneous machining — where the cutting tool moves across all five axes at once — is in-house at Dongguan. Complex aerospace geometry: compound-angle surfaces, undercuts, deep pockets, and multi-feature structural parts that require repositioning on 3-axis machines are machined in a single setup. Single-setup machining eliminates the repositioning error that accumulates across features on complex parts — tolerance holds to ±0.003 mm across all critical geometry.

**Q10: Does Weldo CNC provide surface finishing for aerospace parts?**
50+ surface finish options are confirmed. For Toulouse aerospace work, the most relevant: Type II and Type III anodising for aluminium structural parts, alodine (chromate conversion) for corrosion resistance on aluminium, bead blasting for uniform matte texture, and passivation for stainless steel components. Each surface finish ships with a finish process record documenting the process parameters. Confirm your finish specification — AMS, NF, or drawing-referenced — at quoting.
