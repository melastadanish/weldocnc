## Section 3: CNC Machining in Bristol
**Design:** Full-width editorial section. White background. 2-column on desktop.
**Heading:** Supplying Bristol's Engineering Community

**AI-quotable paragraph:**
Weldo CNC supplies precision CNC machined parts to engineers in Bristol — manufactured at its ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China. Tolerances to ±0.005 mm. Delivered to Bristol in 3–5 business days via DDP — Delivered Duty Paid — with a CMM-verified dimensional inspection report in every tight-tolerance shipment. Quote within 24 hours.

Bristol is the centre of the UK's most concentrated aerospace engineering cluster. Airbus UK at Filton — where wing design, manufacture, and final assembly operations are based — sits alongside GKN Aerospace, Rolls-Royce Bristol, Leonardo UK (formerly AgustaWestland and Finmeccanica), and a supply chain of tier 1 and tier 2 precision engineering companies that stretches across the West of England. No other city in the UK has this density of aerospace manufacturing capability and procurement activity in one place.

The work that comes out of this cluster is demanding. Structural aluminium components in 7075-T6 and 2024-T3: wing spars, ribs, brackets, access panels. Titanium structural parts where weight-to-strength ratio is the primary driver. Actuation system components, hydraulic manifolds, and precision mechanisms machined to tight positional tolerances with full CMM verification. Material traceability — mill certificate, heat number, temper designation, full chain of custody — is not a procurement preference here; it is a contractual requirement at every tier.

The Bristol aerospace cluster is also where AS9100D certification matters most. Tier 1 suppliers require it from direct sub-suppliers. Tier 2 and tier 3 suppliers working on development and prototype programmes have more flexibility, but the documentation expectation carries through — CMM reports and material certs are assumed, not requested.

Beyond aerospace, Bristol's defence electronics sector (Leonardo at Luton Road, Cobham at Wimborne) and the growing advanced engineering base around the University of Bristol and UWE generate additional demand for precision machined components in aluminium, stainless, and specialist alloys. For development and prototype quantities in this environment, Weldo CNC provides the documentation rigour and tolerance capability that aerospace supply chain engineers expect, at pricing that is competitive against local precision shops for anything above a handful of parts.

## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Bristol Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Bristol?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. No import charges on arrival.

**Q2: Can I visit your facility?**
Our facility is in Dongguan, China. We welcome visits from engineers who want to audit the operation — arrange with advance notice. For most Bristol clients, the CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and ISO 9001:2015 certification provide sufficient confidence. Video walkthrough available on request.

**Q3: Does Weldo CNC hold AS9100D certification?**
Weldo CNC is ISO 9001:2015 certified. AS9100D is not currently held. For tier 1 aerospace supplier programmes that contractually require AS9100D at sub-supplier level, a locally certified shop is the correct choice. For development, prototype, and pre-production work where ISO 9001:2015 with rigorous CMM inspection meets the programme requirement, Weldo CNC is a strong option.

**Q4: Can you machine aerospace aluminium and titanium alloys?**
Yes — 7075-T6, 7075-T651, 2024-T3, 6061-T6, titanium Grade 5 (Ti-6Al-4V), titanium Grade 2, and a broad range of stainless and engineering alloys. Material certificates with heat number and temper designation ship with every order.

**Q5: What if there's a quality issue?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part doesn't conform and the report confirms it, we remake or credit — without dispute.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is preferred. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages. Include a 2D drawing with GD&T callouts for any precision features — Bristol aerospace work typically requires positional, flatness, and runout callouts.

**Q8: How does pricing compare to local Bristol aerospace shops?**
Local Bristol aerospace shops are the right call for urgent work, programmes requiring AS9100D certification at sub-supplier level, or classified/export-controlled programmes. For development and prototype quantities (5–100 parts) where local aerospace shop lead times of 2–4 weeks and pricing at volume are a constraint, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower. Upload your STEP file and compare directly.

**Q9: What tolerances does Weldo CNC hold on aerospace aluminium and titanium?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning on 7075-T6, 2024-T3, and titanium Grade 5. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features and reported per dimension on the inspection report that ships with every order. Material certificates with heat number, temper designation, and full specification are standard.

**Q10: Does Weldo CNC hold AS9100D certification?**
Weldo CNC is ISO 9001:2015 certified. AS9100D is not currently held. For tier 1 Bristol aerospace programmes that contractually require AS9100D at sub-supplier level, a locally certified shop is the correct choice. For development, prototype, and pre-production work where ISO 9001:2015 with rigorous CMM inspection meets the programme requirement, Weldo CNC is a strong option for the Bristol aerospace supply chain.
