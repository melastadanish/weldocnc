## Section 10: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Cambridge Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Cambridge?**
Parts ship from Dongguan, China via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. Transit to Cambridge takes 3–5 business days. Add 1–5 business days for prototype manufacturing — total delivery is 4–8 business days for most prototype and small-batch orders. All shipments are DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) — UK import duties and customs clearance are handled and included in the quoted price. Parts arrive at your Cambridge address with no additional charges.

**Q2: What are the post-Brexit import duties on CNC parts shipped to Cambridge?**
CNC machined parts typically fall under commodity code 8466.93 (UK Global Tariff). Import duty is 0–2.7% depending on material and part classification. Weldo CNC ships DDP — the duty is paid before the shipment leaves China, included in your quoted price. For VAT-registered UK businesses, 20% import VAT is applied and reclaimed through postponed VAT accounting — no cash-flow impact.

**Q3: What tolerance does Weldo CNC hold on medtech and scientific instrument parts?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry — multi-axis instrument housings, compound-angle features — ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features. The inspection report ships with every order, reporting each critical dimension against your drawing callout. Cambridge medtech and scientific instrument work typically calls for ±0.005–0.020 mm; Weldo CNC covers this range in full.

**Q4: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Cambridge precision shops?**
Cambridge-area shops are suited to urgent bench-test deadlines and programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier. Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice for tight-tolerance work (±0.005–0.025 mm), full material documentation, specialist alloys (titanium Ti-6Al-4V, PEEK, PTFE, 17-4PH), or quantities above 5 parts where local pricing becomes significant. Cambridge spinouts typically find the 4–8 day DDP total lead time fits development schedules well. Upload your drawing and compare quotes directly.

**Q5: Do you work with Cambridge Biomedical Campus and medtech companies?**
Yes — surgical instrument prototypes, diagnostic device housings, laboratory instrument components, and implant-adjacent hardware in 316L stainless steel and titanium Ti-6Al-4V are a significant part of the Cambridge workload. Full material traceability — heat number, temper, and material specification — is standard on every order. For ISO 13485 programmes or parts requiring ISO 10993 biocompatibility documentation consideration, confirm your specific requirements at the quoting stage.

**Q6: What materials does Weldo CNC machine for Cambridge's science and medtech buyers?**
100+ machinable materials — including stainless steel 316L for medical-grade components; titanium Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) for implant-adjacent and high-strength hardware; PEEK and PTFE for medical and laboratory plastics; aluminium 6061-T6 and 7075-T6 for optical mounts and instrument housings; and 17-4PH stainless for high-strength precision components. Electropolishing and passivation are available for stainless steel surface finishing. Confirm your specific grade at the quoting stage.

**Q7: Is there a minimum order quantity for Cambridge customers?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities — and Cambridge spinout teams commonly start with a single prototype. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed across the order. There is no floor on order size; one PEEK instrument housing quotes the same way as a 100-piece production build.

**Q8: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for Cambridge quotes?**
STEP is the preferred format — it carries the 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and toolpath programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For any precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Inch and metric drawings are both accepted; BSP threads quoted alongside M-series.

**Q9: Can Weldo CNC supply robotics and automation companies in Cambridge?**
Yes — actuator housings, joint mechanisms, gripper bodies, and sensor mount hardware for robotics applications are a confirmed workload. Small batches — 1–10 parts — with fast turnaround on design revisions are handled without minimum order requirements. The 24-hour quote and 4–8 day DDP total delivery matches how Cambridge robotics teams iterate: fast design cycles with parts arriving before the next build session.

**Q10: How does pricing compare to Cambridge precision shops for research and spinout buyers?**
For a single urgent part needed within 24–48 hours, a local Cambridge shop offers the advantage of same-day collection. From 5–10 parts upward, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower on unit cost — no local overhead, and specialist alloys or 5-axis geometry carry no Cambridge premium.
