## Section 10: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Manchester Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Manchester?**
Parts ship from Dongguan, China via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. Transit to Manchester takes 3–5 business days. Add 1–5 business days for prototype manufacturing — total delivery is 4–8 business days for most prototype and small-batch orders. All shipments are DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) — UK import duties and customs clearance are handled and included in the quoted price. Parts arrive at your Manchester address with no additional charges.

**Q2: What are the post-Brexit import duties on CNC parts shipped to Manchester?**
CNC machined parts typically fall under commodity code 8466.93 (UK Global Tariff). Import duty is 0–2.7% depending on material and part classification. Weldo CNC ships DDP — duty is paid before the shipment leaves China, included in your quoted price. For VAT-registered UK businesses, 20% import VAT is applied and reclaimed through postponed VAT accounting — no cash-flow impact.

**Q3: What tolerance does Weldo CNC hold on aerospace parts for Manchester's supply chain?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features. The inspection report ships with every order, reporting each critical dimension against your drawing callout. BS EN ISO 2768 general tolerances apply as baseline; tighter callouts are quoted per drawing.

**Q4: How does Weldo CNC compare to Manchester and North West CNC shops?**
North West shops are well suited to urgent jobs and AS9100D programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier. Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice for tight-tolerance work (±0.005–0.025 mm), full CMM documentation, specialist alloys (titanium Ti-6Al-4V, duplex stainless), complex 5-axis geometry, or quantities above 10 parts where North West pricing becomes significant. Upload your drawing — you can compare quotes directly.

**Q5: Can Weldo CNC supply aerospace Tier 2 and Tier 3 buyers in the Manchester region?**
Yes — aluminium structural components, titanium brackets and housings, avionics enclosures, and complex 5-axis geometry for aerospace assemblies are a confirmed workload. Full material traceability — heat number, temper, and material specification — is standard on every order. CMM-verified dimensional reports ship with every tight-tolerance order. For AS9100D programme requirements, confirm specific documentation requirements at the quoting stage.

**Q6: What materials does Weldo CNC machine for Manchester's aerospace and defence buyers?**
100+ machinable materials — including aluminium 6061-T6, 7075-T6, and 2024 for aerospace structural work; titanium Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) for structural brackets and housings; stainless steel 304, 316L, and 17-4PH for precision components; and duplex stainless for energy and nuclear-adjacent applications. PEEK and engineering plastics are also machined. Confirm your specific grade at the quoting stage.

**Q7: Is there a minimum order quantity for Manchester customers?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities — prototype runs start at a single part. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed across the order. There is no floor on order size.

**Q8: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for Manchester quotes?**
STEP is the preferred format — it carries the 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and toolpath programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For any precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Inch and metric drawings are both accepted; BSP threads quoted alongside M-series.

**Q9: Does Weldo CNC work with Manchester digital health and medtech companies?**
Yes — stainless steel and titanium CNC machined components for medical device prototypes and diagnostic hardware are a confirmed workload. Full material traceability is standard on every order. For ISO 13485 programmes, confirm specific documentation requirements at the quoting stage. University of Manchester spinouts and Manchester Science Park companies have direct access to the same quoting process — no minimum order, no qualification delay.

**Q10: How does pricing compare to local Manchester machine shops at volume?**
For a single urgent part needed within 24–48 hours, a local North West shop may be competitive with the advantage of same-day collection. From 10–20 parts upward, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower on unit cost — no regional overhead, and titanium or 5-axis geometry carry no North West premium. For 50–200 part runs with full CMM documentation, the difference is significant. Upload your STEP file and compare.
