## Section 10: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Birmingham Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Birmingham?**
Parts ship from Dongguan, China via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. Transit to Birmingham takes 3–5 business days. Add 1–5 business days for prototype manufacturing — total delivery is 4–8 business days for most prototype and small-batch orders. All shipments are DDP (Delivered Duty Paid) — UK import duties and customs clearance are handled and included in the quoted price. Parts arrive at your Birmingham address with no additional charges.

**Q2: What are the post-Brexit import duties on CNC parts shipped to Birmingham?**
CNC machined parts typically fall under commodity code 8466.93 (UK Global Tariff). Import duty is 0–2.7% depending on material and part classification. Weldo CNC ships DDP — the duty is paid before the shipment leaves China, included in your quoted price. For VAT-registered UK businesses, 20% import VAT is applied and reclaimed through postponed VAT accounting — no cash-flow impact.

**Q3: What tolerance does Weldo CNC hold on automotive and aerospace parts?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features. The inspection report ships with every order, reporting each critical dimension against your drawing callout. BS EN ISO 2768 general tolerances are applied as the baseline where drawing callouts are not specified.

**Q4: How does Weldo CNC compare to West Midlands precision machine shops?**
West Midlands shops are well suited to urgent jobs and IATF 16949 programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier. Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice for tight-tolerance work (±0.005–0.025 mm), full CMM documentation, specialist alloys (titanium, 17-4PH, 4140 alloy steel), complex 5-axis geometry, or quantities above 10 parts where West Midlands pricing becomes significant. Upload your drawing — you can compare quotes directly.

**Q5: Can Weldo CNC supply the JLR and automotive supply chain in Birmingham?**
Weldo CNC supplies CNC machined parts for automotive development and pre-production programmes — engine components, transmission housings, suspension geometry parts, and EV powertrain components in aluminium, steel, and specialist alloys. Full material traceability and CMM documentation are standard on every order. For IATF 16949 programmes requiring a locally certified supplier, confirm your specific programme requirements before placing an order.

**Q6: What materials does Weldo CNC machine for Birmingham's aerospace and automotive buyers?**
100+ machinable materials — including aluminium 6061-T6, 7075-T6, and 2024 for aerospace structural work; stainless steel 304, 316L, and 17-4PH for precision components; titanium Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) for aerospace and high-performance automotive; and alloy steels 4130, 4140, and 4340 for high-strength structural components. PEEK and engineering plastics are also machined. Confirm your specific grade at the quoting stage.

**Q7: Is there a minimum order quantity for Birmingham customers?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities — prototype runs start at a single part. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed across the order. There is no floor on order size; a single steel bracket quotes the same way as a 500-piece batch.

**Q8: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for Birmingham quotes?**
STEP is the preferred format — it carries the 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and toolpath programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For any precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Inch and metric drawings are both accepted; BSP threads quoted alongside M-series.

**Q9: How does pricing compare to local Birmingham machine shops at volume?**
For a single urgent part needed within 24–48 hours, a local West Midlands shop may be competitive with the advantage of same-day collection. From 10–20 parts upward, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower on unit cost — no local overhead, and specialist alloys or complex geometry carry no regional premium. For 50–200 part runs with full CMM documentation, the cost difference is significant. Upload your STEP file and compare directly.

**Q10: Does Weldo CNC work with Birmingham medical device companies?**
Yes — stainless steel and titanium CNC machined components for medical devices and diagnostic equipment are a confirmed workload. Full material traceability — heat number, temper, and material specification — is standard on every order. For ISO 13485 programmes, confirm specific documentation requirements at the quoting stage. BS EN ISO 2768 general tolerances apply as standard; tighter callouts are quoted per drawing.
