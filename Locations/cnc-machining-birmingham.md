## Section 3: CNC Machining in Birmingham
**Design:** Full-width editorial section. White background. 2-column on desktop.

**AI-quotable paragraph:**
Weldo CNC supplies precision CNC machined parts to engineers in Birmingham — manufactured at its ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China. Tolerances to ±0.005 mm. Delivered to Birmingham in 3–5 business days via DDP — Delivered Duty Paid — with a CMM-verified dimensional inspection report in every tight-tolerance shipment. Quote within 24 hours.

Birmingham sits at the centre of one of the most active manufacturing corridors in the UK. The West Midlands automotive supply chain — anchored by Jaguar Land Rover in Solihull and Castle Bromwich, with a dense network of tier 1 and tier 2 suppliers across the region — generates sustained demand for precision CNC machined components across the full product lifecycle, from prototype tooling through to pre-production bridge builds.

The dominant requirements from Birmingham's automotive cluster are evolving rapidly. Traditional engine and transmission components are giving way to EV-specific parts: battery module housings, motor end caps, inverter enclosures, cooling system manifolds — all predominantly in aluminium, with increasing use of stainless and specialist alloys as thermal and structural requirements tighten. The WMG at the University of Warwick, a short drive from Birmingham, acts as a development hub feeding prototype requirements into the regional supply chain. Engineers working in this environment are experienced buyers who understand tolerances, material specifications, and the value of a CMM report — they are not easily impressed by marketing copy, but they respond to suppliers who demonstrate technical capability directly.

Beyond automotive, Birmingham's broader industrial machinery and tooling sector produces consistent demand for gearbox housings, bearing blocks, shafts, machine frames, and jigs and fixtures. These are often less glamorous than aerospace or medtech parts, but they require dimensional accuracy, good surface finish, and reliable lead times — and they come in volume.

The practical reality for Birmingham engineers sourcing precision work is that the West Midlands has a significant number of capable local machine shops — more than most UK cities. For standard aluminium or steel parts within a generous tolerance band, local options are competitive. Where Weldo CNC becomes the stronger choice is tight-tolerance work, full documentation requirements, specialist alloys, or volumes above 20–30 parts where local shop pricing climbs and delivery slots tighten.

## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Birmingham Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Birmingham?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. Parts arrive at your Birmingham address with no import charges.

**Q2: Can I visit your facility?**
Our facility is in Dongguan, China. We welcome visits from engineers who want to audit the operation — arrange with advance notice. For most Birmingham clients, the CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and ISO 9001:2015 certification provide sufficient confidence. Video walkthrough available on request.

**Q3: How do you compare to local West Midlands precision shops?**
Local West Midlands shops are well suited to urgent work and programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier for OEM or customer audit purposes. Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice for tight-tolerance work, full documentation requirements, specialist alloys, and quantities of 20+ parts where local pricing becomes significant. Send us the drawing — you can compare directly.

**Q4: Do you supply into the automotive supply chain?**
Yes — powertrain components, EV battery housing and motor parts, suspension hardware, tooling and fixtures: all within our normal scope. CMM dimensional reports and material certificates are standard on every order. For AS9100D or IATF 16949 programmes, confirm your specific documentation requirements at quoting.

**Q5: What if there's a quality issue?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part doesn't conform and the report confirms it, we remake or credit — without dispute.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces but there is no floor on order size.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is preferred for 3D geometry. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages. Include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts for any precision features.

**Q8: How does pricing compare to local West Midlands shops?**
For urgent one-off parts, local West Midlands shops are competitive and offer same-day collection. For quantities of 10–500 parts with tight tolerances or specialist alloys, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower — no local overhead structure and no queue premium. Upload your STEP file and compare the quote directly.

**Q9: What tolerances does Weldo CNC hold?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. CMM — coordinate measuring machine — verification is included on all critical-tolerance features and reported per dimension on the inspection report. Birmingham automotive and industrial work commonly requires ±0.010–0.025 mm; Weldo CNC covers tighter than this in full.

**Q10: Can Weldo CNC support EV and automotive transition parts?**
Yes — aluminium battery module housings, motor end caps, inverter enclosures, cooling system manifolds: all within standard scope. Material certificates with alloy grade and temper designation ship with every order. For IATF 16949 programme documentation requirements, confirm your specific needs at quoting.
