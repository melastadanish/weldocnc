## Section 3: CNC Machining in London
**Design:** Full-width editorial section. White background. 2-column on desktop.
**Heading:** Supplying London's Engineering Community

London's engineering community is concentrated in two distinct clusters, each with different requirements and a different relationship with precision CNC machining.

The medtech and life sciences cluster — centred around King's Cross, the satellite campuses of Imperial College and UCL, and the growing biomedical precinct at White City — generates consistent demand for ISO 9001:2015 compliant CNC machined stainless steel and titanium components. Surgical instrument prototypes, diagnostic device housings, implant-adjacent hardware, microfluidic manifolds: these are parts where full material traceability and CMM reporting are expected from the first enquiry, not requested as a premium. The engineers specifying this work typically have a detailed understanding of what they're asking for and will read the inspection report.

The deep tech and robotics community across East London, the Old Street and Shoreditch corridor, and the expanding cluster around King's Cross and the Crick Institute produces a different requirement: rapid iteration on aluminium structural parts, actuator housings, sensor mounts, precision mechanisms, and end-effector tooling. Geometry changes frequently. Tolerances remain tight. The need is for a supplier who can quote a new revision in 24 hours and turn aluminium parts in 3–5 business days without a lengthy qualification process every time.

London-area precision machine shops are capable and handle urgent work well. If you need a part in 24–48 hours for a critical test or a last-minute demo, a local shop is the right answer — transit time from Dongguan cannot compete. Where the economics and capability shift is at tighter tolerances, complex 5-axis geometry, specialist alloys, or quantities above a handful of parts. At that point, local precision shop lead times (typically 2–4 weeks), pricing at volume, and variable documentation standards make offshore sourcing the practical choice for most London development and pre-production programmes.

The other common pattern in London is engineers who use both: a local shop for urgent one-offs, and Weldo CNC for the production intent build or the 50-part pre-production run that needs to come in with full documentation.

## Section 10: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for London Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to London?**
3–5 business days from Dongguan via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. All shipments are DDP — duties and customs clearance handled, included in the quoted price. Parts arrive at your London address with no import charges and no customs administration on your end.

**Q2: Can I visit your facility?**
Our facility is in Dongguan, China. We welcome visits from engineers who want to audit the operation — arrange with advance notice. For most London clients, the CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and ISO 9001:2015 certification provide sufficient confidence without travel. Video walkthrough available on request.

**Q3: How do you compare to local London precision shops?**
Local London shops are the right answer for urgent 24–48 hour jobs and programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier. Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice for tight-tolerance work (±0.005–0.025mm), full documentation requirements, specialist alloys, complex 5-axis geometry, or quantities above 10 parts where local pricing becomes significant. Send us the drawing and we'll quote — you can compare directly.

**Q4: What if there's a quality issue?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part doesn't conform and the report confirms it, we remake or credit — without dispute. Non-conformances are caught at our inspection stage before parts leave the facility.

**Q5: Do you work with London medtech and life sciences companies?**
Yes — this is a significant part of our London workload. Surgical instrument prototypes, diagnostic device housings, implant-adjacent hardware in 316L and titanium: full material traceability and CMM documentation are standard on every order, not requested as an add-on.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One piece to production quantities. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces — setup cost is fixed — but there is no floor on order size.
