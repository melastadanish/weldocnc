## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Stuttgart Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Stuttgart?**
3–5 business days shipping from Dongguan to Stuttgart, after 1–5 business days manufacturing — total 4–10 business days from order confirmation to your Stuttgart address. All shipments are DDP — duties, German customs clearance, and VAT handled and included in the price. Tracking provided at dispatch via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority.

**Q2: What import duties apply when shipping CNC parts to Stuttgart?**
CNC machined parts typically fall under CN commodity code 8466.93. The EU import duty rate is 2.7–3.7% of the declared goods value. Weldo CNC ships DDP — all duty and clearance costs are priced in. German VAT at 19% is applied at customs and is fully reclaimable if your business is VAT registered in Germany. No broker fees, no additional import invoices on arrival.

**Q3: What tolerance standard does Weldo CNC apply to Stuttgart automotive drawings?**
Metric drawings are manufactured to DIN EN ISO 2768 tolerance standards — the same standard used across Stuttgart's automotive and precision engineering supply chains. General tolerances default to ISO 2768-m (medium) for metals. Threaded features are machined to M-thread DIN 13. Tighter featural tolerances — ±0.005 mm and below — are applied as called out on the drawing. Submit your drawing with DIN callouts and the quote is based on those requirements.

**Q4: Can Weldo CNC supply into Stuttgart's Mercedes-Benz or Bosch supply chains?**
Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification. IATF 16949 certification is not held. For programmes requiring IATF 16949 approved supplier status, confirm with your programme quality team whether ISO 9001:2015 documentation plus CMM-verified dimensional reports satisfies incoming inspection. The CMM report, material certificate, and finish record that ship standard with every Weldo CNC order meet many first-article and receiving inspection requirements. Stuttgart procurement teams with experience of offshore sourcing typically confirm documentation requirements at quoting — which is the right approach.

**Q5: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Stuttgart precision shops?**
Stuttgart's local Mittelstand precision shops are experienced and capable — this is one of Germany's strongest precision engineering clusters. Local shops are the right answer for urgent same-day requirements and for programmes requiring an IATF 16949 auditable local supplier. Weldo CNC becomes the stronger choice for quantities above 10–20 parts, full documentation on every piece, specialist alloys, 5-axis geometry, or cost-sensitive pre-production batches. Submit your drawing — quote in 24 hours for direct comparison.

**Q6: What materials does Weldo CNC machine for Stuttgart's automotive and industrial customers?**
100+ metals and engineering plastics. For Stuttgart's primary industries: aluminium 7075-T6 and 6061-T6 for structural automotive and industrial parts; stainless steel 304, 316, and 17-4PH for precision mechanisms; titanium Ti-6Al-4V for weight-critical applications; tool steel H13 and D2 for tooling and die components; PEEK and engineering polymers for high-temperature or chemical-resistant parts. Confirm your grade at quoting — the quote specifies the material and a certificate ships with every order.

**Q7: Is there a minimum order quantity for CNC parts shipped to Stuttgart?**
No minimum. Single prototype pieces to production quantities — all accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed regardless of quantity, but there is no floor on order size. Stuttgart automotive and industrial engineers routinely order 1–5 first-article parts, then scale to 50–200 part pre-production batches with the same documentation pack.

**Q8: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is preferred — it carries 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings and DIN callouts are handled without conversion.

**Q9: What if there is a quality issue with parts delivered to Stuttgart?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part does not conform and the report confirms the deviation, Weldo CNC remakes or credits — without dispute. Non-conformances are caught before parts leave the Dongguan facility. The 97.48% annual pass rate reflects the three-stage inspection process: pre-production, in-production, and final inspection.

**Q10: How does Weldo CNC handle MEMS and micro-feature precision parts for Stuttgart's research and automation sector?**
Small-feature milling and tight-bore turning are within standard capability. Minimum end mill diameter is 0.5 mm. Wall thickness from 0.5 mm. Tolerances to ±0.005 mm on milled features; ±0.003 mm on 5-axis simultaneous geometry. Ra 0.8 µm surface finish is achievable on finished surfaces. For Stuttgart MEMS, microsystems, and automation engineering customers, the key question is feature size and geometry — submit your STEP file with a 2D drawing and the quote will confirm exactly what is achievable on your specific part.
