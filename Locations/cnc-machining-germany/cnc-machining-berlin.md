Weldo CNC supplies precision CNC machined parts to engineers in Berlin — manufactured at its ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China. Tolerances to ±0.005 mm, DIN EN ISO 2768 compliant, delivered to Berlin in 4–10 business days via DDP — Delivered Duty Paid. Every tight-tolerance shipment includes a CMM-verified dimensional inspection report. Quote within 24 hours.

Berlin sits at an unusual intersection for precision manufacturing. Germany's startup capital is simultaneously home to one of Europe's most active medtech clusters, a growing defence electronics scene, and a significant number of university spinouts from TU Berlin and the Charité university hospital. The result is an engineering buyer community that ranges from early-stage hardware startups iterating through prototypes every two weeks to established research institutions with precise documentation requirements on every order.

The startup and tech hardware segment — concentrated across Mitte, Prenzlauer Berg, and the expanding Adlershof technology park — generates high-frequency demand for aluminium structural parts, sensor housings, actuator brackets, and custom electronics enclosures. These buyers typically need fast revision cycles and no minimum order commitment. A single prototype ordered on Monday needs to land on a Berlin workbench by end of the following week. That requirement shapes everything: quote speed, production lead time, and the reliability of the delivery window.

Berlin's medtech buyers — many connected to Charité, one of Europe's largest university hospitals, and to the growing network of life sciences firms that has clustered around the campus — bring a different set of requirements. Stainless steel 316L and titanium Ti-6Al-4V are the common materials. Full material traceability, heat number documentation, and dimensional reports verified against the drawing are expected as standard, not as a premium add-on. DIN EN ISO 2768 medium-class tolerance callouts are common; tighter featural tolerances on critical interfaces are routinely specified to ±0.01–0.02 mm.

Local Berlin CNC shops — largely smaller Mittelstand operations and a handful of engineering service bureaus — handle urgent work competently. For parts needed in 24–48 hours, they are the right answer. For tighter tolerance work requiring full documentation, specialist alloys, 5-axis geometry, or quantities above ten parts where local pricing becomes significant, the economics of offshore sourcing shift clearly. Berlin's startup buyers have discovered this early: iterating on twenty aluminium prototypes with a supplier in Dongguan costs less and takes less calendar time than queuing at an oversubscribed local workshop.

## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Berlin Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Berlin?**
3–5 business days shipping from Dongguan to Berlin, after 1–5 business days manufacturing — total 4–10 business days from order confirmation to your Berlin address. All shipments are DDP — Delivered Duty Paid — meaning duties, German customs clearance, and VAT are handled and included in the quoted price. No import charges arrive separately. Tracking is provided at dispatch via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority.

**Q2: What import duties apply to CNC parts shipped to Berlin?**
CNC machined parts typically fall under CN commodity code 8466.93. The EU import duty rate for this code is 2.7–3.7% of the declared goods value. Weldo CNC ships DDP — all duty and customs clearance costs are included in your price. German VAT at 19% is applied at customs and is fully reclaimable if your business is VAT registered in Germany. No broker fees, no surprises on arrival.

**Q3: What tolerance standard do you use — DIN or ISO?**
Drawings submitted in metric format are quoted and manufactured to DIN EN ISO 2768 tolerance standards — the same standard used across German and EU precision engineering. General tolerances default to ISO 2768-m (medium) for metals. Threaded features are machined to M-thread DIN 13. Tighter featural tolerances — ±0.005 mm and below — are applied as called out on the drawing. If your drawing uses DIN callouts, submit it as-is and the quote will be based on those requirements.

**Q4: Can Weldo CNC supply Berlin medtech and life sciences companies?**
Yes — surgical instrument prototypes, diagnostic device housings, and implant-adjacent hardware in 316L stainless and titanium Ti-6Al-4V are part of the Berlin workload. Full material traceability — heat number, temper, and specification — and CMM dimensional reports are standard on every order, not optional extras. For ISO 13485 programme requirements, specify your documentation needs at quoting stage and the quote will confirm what is covered.

**Q5: How does Weldo CNC compare to Berlin local CNC shops?**
Local Berlin workshops are the right answer for urgent 24–48 hour jobs and programmes requiring an on-site auditable supplier. Weldo CNC is typically the stronger choice for tight tolerances (±0.005–0.02 mm), full documentation requirements, specialist alloys, complex 5-axis geometry, or quantities above ten parts where local pricing becomes significant. Send the drawing — we'll quote in 24 hours and you can compare directly.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity for CNC parts delivered to Berlin?**
No minimum. Single prototype pieces to production quantities — all accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed regardless of quantity, but there is no floor on order size. Berlin startup customers regularly order one to five parts for initial build and validation, then scale to larger batches for pre-production.

**Q7: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is the preferred format — it carries 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For any precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings with DIN callouts are handled without conversion.

**Q8: What if there is a quality issue with parts delivered to Berlin?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part does not conform and the report confirms the deviation, Weldo CNC remakes or credits — without dispute. Non-conformances are caught at the in-house inspection stage before parts leave the Dongguan facility. The 97.48% annual pass rate reflects the effectiveness of the three-stage inspection process: pre-production, in-production, and final.

**Q9: What materials does Weldo CNC machine for Berlin customers?**
100+ metals and engineering plastics are confirmed. For Berlin's primary industries: aluminium 6061-T6 and 7075-T6 for hardware and electronics enclosures; stainless steel 316L and 17-4PH for medtech and defence components; titanium Ti-6Al-4V for weight-critical and biocompatible parts; PEEK for high-temperature or chemical-resistant housings. Confirm your specific grade at quoting — the quote will specify the material used and supply a certificate with the order.

**Q10: How does pricing compare to local Berlin machine shops?**
For a single urgent part needed in 24–48 hours, a local Berlin shop may be competitive and offers same-day collection. From 5–10 parts upward, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower on unit cost — no local overhead structure, and complex geometry or specialist alloys are priced without the Berlin workshop premium. For quantities of 50–200 parts with full documentation, the cost difference is significant. Upload your STEP file and compare directly — the quote is free and arrives within 24 hours.
