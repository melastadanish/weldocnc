## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Frankfurt Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Frankfurt?**
3–5 business days shipping from Dongguan to Frankfurt, after 1–5 business days manufacturing — total 4–10 business days from order confirmation to your Frankfurt address. All shipments are DDP — duties, German customs clearance, and VAT handled and included in the price. Frankfurt Airport is one of Europe's main air freight hubs, which means DHL Express and FedEx shipments from China clear through a high-throughput, efficient customs environment. Tracking is provided at dispatch.

**Q2: What import duties apply when shipping CNC parts to Frankfurt?**
CNC machined parts typically fall under CN commodity code 8466.93. The EU import duty rate for this code is 2.7–3.7% of the declared goods value. Weldo CNC ships DDP — all duty and clearance costs are priced in. German VAT at 19% is applied at customs and is fully reclaimable if your business is VAT registered in Germany. No broker fees, no additional invoices on arrival.

**Q3: Can Weldo CNC deliver trade fair prototypes to Frankfurt in time for Messe Frankfurt exhibitions?**
Yes — with adequate planning lead time. The total DDP window from order confirmation to Frankfurt delivery is 4–10 business days. For a Messe Frankfurt exhibition, the practical approach is to order prototype parts 3–4 weeks before the show opens — that window covers manufacturing, inspection, and delivery with margin for a single revision cycle if required. For parts ordered fewer than 10 business days before the show, confirm available production slots at quoting. Rush manufacturing is available on selected geometries.

**Q4: What tolerances can Weldo CNC hold on pharmaceutical and analytical instrument components?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry — reactor fittings, analytical housings, multi-axis valve bodies — ±0.003 mm is achievable. CMM verification is included on all critical-tolerance features and reported per dimension on the inspection report shipped with every order. Stainless steel 316L — the default material for pharmaceutical contact surfaces — machines to ±0.005 mm with consistent surface finish documentation.

**Q5: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Frankfurt precision shops?**
Local Frankfurt shops are the right answer for urgent 24–48 hour requirements and for programmes requiring a locally auditable supplier. Weldo CNC becomes the stronger choice for tight tolerances, full documentation on every piece, specialist alloys (316L, titanium, PEEK), or quantities above 10 parts where local pricing becomes significant. Submit your drawing — quote in 24 hours for direct comparison.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity for CNC parts shipped to Frankfurt?**
No minimum. Single prototype pieces to production quantities — all accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed regardless of quantity, but there is no floor on order size. Frankfurt pharma and medtech buyers regularly order 1–5 first-article parts for validation, then scale to larger batches with the same documentation pack.

**Q7: What materials does Weldo CNC machine for Frankfurt's pharmaceutical and medtech industries?**
100+ metals and engineering plastics. For Frankfurt's primary industries: stainless steel 316L for pharmaceutical contact surfaces and medical device components; titanium Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) for weight-critical medical applications; PEEK for biocompatible and chemical-resistant housings; aluminium 6061-T6 for instrument enclosures and structural components; stainless steel 17-4PH for high-strength precision assemblies. Confirm your grade at quoting — a material certificate ships with every order.

**Q8: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is preferred — it carries 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings and DIN callouts are handled without conversion.

**Q9: What if there is a quality issue with parts delivered to Frankfurt?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part does not conform and the report confirms the deviation, Weldo CNC remakes or credits — without dispute. Non-conformances are caught before parts leave the Dongguan facility. The 97.48% annual pass rate reflects the three-stage inspection process: pre-production, in-production, and final inspection.

**Q10: Can Weldo CNC visit Frankfurt for a supplier qualification or meeting?**
Weldo CNC's facility is in Dongguan, China. No physical presence in Frankfurt. All communication is remote in English and German-language support can be arranged. For engineers who prefer a structured conversation, video calls are scheduled without issue across the time zone. A video walkthrough of the Dongguan facility is available on request — suitable for most initial supplier qualification checks.
