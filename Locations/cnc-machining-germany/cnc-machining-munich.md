## Section 11: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background. First item open.
**Heading:** CNC Machining for Munich Engineers — FAQs

**Q1: How long does delivery take from China to Munich?**
3–5 business days shipping from Dongguan to Munich, after 1–5 business days manufacturing — total 4–10 business days from order confirmation to your Munich address. All shipments are DDP — Delivered Duty Paid — duties, German customs clearance, and VAT are handled and included in the price. Tracking is provided at dispatch via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority.

**Q2: What import duties apply when shipping CNC parts to Munich?**
CNC machined parts typically fall under CN commodity code 8466.93. The EU import duty rate for this code is 2.7–3.7% of the declared goods value. Weldo CNC ships DDP — all duty and clearance costs are priced in. German VAT at 19% is applied at customs and is fully reclaimable if your business is VAT registered in Germany.

**Q3: Can Weldo CNC supply parts into IATF 16949 automotive programmes?**
Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification. If you are sourcing for an IATF 16949 controlled programme — common among Munich's BMW Group supply chain — confirm your specific documentation requirements at quoting. The CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and dimensional inspection record that ship standard with every Weldo CNC order satisfy many first-article inspection and incoming quality check requirements. For full IATF 16949 approved supplier status, confirm with your programme quality team before placing orders.

**Q4: What tolerances can Weldo CNC hold on automotive and aerospace parts?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry — compressor housings, aerospace fixtures, precision mounts — ±0.003 mm is achievable. CMM verification is included on all critical-tolerance features and reported per dimension on the inspection report shipped with the parts. Munich automotive and aerospace buyers routinely call for tolerances in the ±0.005–0.02 mm range; Weldo CNC covers this in full.

**Q5: How does Weldo CNC compare to local Munich precision shops?**
Munich's local precision shops are among Germany's best — capable and experienced with demanding tolerance callouts. They are the right choice for urgent same-day or next-day requirements and for programmes requiring an on-site auditable supplier. Weldo CNC becomes the stronger choice at quantities above 10–20 parts, for full documentation on every piece, for specialist alloys, and for 5-axis geometry that the smaller Munich workshops price at a significant premium. Submit your drawing — we'll quote in 24 hours for a direct comparison.

**Q6: What DIN tolerance standards does Weldo CNC apply?**
Metric drawings are manufactured to DIN EN ISO 2768 tolerance standards. General tolerances default to ISO 2768-m (medium) for metals. Threaded features are machined to M-thread DIN 13. Tighter featural tolerances are applied as called out on the drawing. Submit your drawing with DIN callouts and the quote will be based on those requirements — no conversion needed.

**Q7: Is there a minimum order quantity for CNC parts shipped to Munich?**
No minimum. Single prototype pieces to production quantities — all accepted. Per-unit cost is higher on single pieces because setup cost is fixed, but there is no floor on order size. Munich automotive and aerospace engineers routinely order 1–5 first-article parts, then scale to pre-production batches of 50–200 with the same supplier and documentation pack.

**Q8: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept?**
STEP is preferred — it carries 3D geometry cleanly for quoting and programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). For any precision features, include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file. Metric drawings and DIN callouts are handled without conversion.

**Q9: What if there is a quality issue with parts delivered to Munich?**
Parts ship with a CMM dimensional report checked against your drawing. If a part does not conform and the report confirms the deviation, Weldo CNC remakes or credits — without dispute. Non-conformances are caught before parts leave the Dongguan facility. The 97.48% annual pass rate reflects the three-stage inspection process: pre-production, in-production, and final. Munich quality engineers who have reviewed the reports routinely confirm they meet incoming inspection expectations.

**Q10: What materials does Weldo CNC machine for Munich's automotive and aerospace industries?**
100+ metals and engineering plastics. For Munich's primary industries: aluminium 7075-T6 and 6061-T6 for structural and chassis components; stainless steel 316L and 17-4PH for precision assemblies; titanium Ti-6Al-4V (Grade 5) for weight-critical and aerospace applications; PEEK and high-performance engineering plastics for thermal and chemical environments. Confirm your specific grade at quoting — the quote specifies the material and a certificate ships with the order.
