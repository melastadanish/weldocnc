What Happens After You Send

**Step 1 — Within 2 Hours:**
Heading: File Received and Acknowledged
Body: Every submission is acknowledged. If a STEP file was included, it goes into the DFM — Design for Manufacturability — review queue immediately. If information is missing — material grade, quantity, tolerance class — a specific question comes back, not a generic "tell us more."

**Step 2 — Within 24 Hours:**
Heading: Quote Returned With DFM Review
Body: Detailed quote with line-item pricing. DFM notes included — any geometry issue that affects machinability, cost, or lead time is flagged with a description and a recommended fix. You decide whether to update the drawing or proceed as-is.

**Step 3 — On Approval:**
Heading: Order Confirmed and Production Scheduled
Body: Order confirmation, production start date, and estimated shipment date. For repeat orders, the previous setup notes and inspection data are on file — lead time may be shorter.

**H2:**
Common Questions Before You Send a File

**Q1 (open by default):**
Q: What file format do you need for a quote?
A: STEP (.stp / .step) is preferred — it carries full 3D geometry and is what toolpaths are generated from. IGES is also accepted. For 2D-only work, a fully dimensioned PDF or DXF is sufficient for simple prismatic geometry. If you only have a sketch or a reference photo, send it — a question about what additional information is needed will follow.

**Q2:**
Q: How quickly will I receive a quote?
A: Within 24 hours of receiving a complete STEP file with material, quantity, and tolerance class. If information is missing, a specific question comes back first — the clock on the 24-hour quote starts when the file is complete.

**Q3:**
Q: Is there a minimum order quantity?
A: No minimum order quantity. Single-part prototype orders are accepted. The same quality process — CMM inspection, material certificate, dimensional report — applies to a one-piece order as to a production run.

**Q4:**
Q: Can Weldo CNC ship to the US or Europe?
A: Yes. Parts ship to customers in 100+ countries. Export documentation, commercial invoices, and DDP — Delivered Duty Paid, meaning the buyer pays no customs fees at destination — freight are available on request.

**Q5:**
Q: What information should I include with my enquiry?
A: STEP file or drawing, material and grade, quantity, tolerance class or specific callouts, required surface finish, and target delivery date if time-sensitive. The more complete the information, the faster and more accurate the quote.
