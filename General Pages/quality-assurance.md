**Subheading:** Every measurement recorded against your drawing revision number — not a generic checklist.

## Section 9: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion, white background, first item open.
**Heading:** Quality Assurance — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerances can Weldo CNC hold and verify on CNC machined parts?**
Standard CNC milling and turning holds tolerances to ±0.05 mm as the fine class per ISO 2768-f. Critical features are verified to ±0.005 mm by CMM — coordinate measuring machine, which measures part geometry to micron-level accuracy. Five-axis machining holds ±0.003 mm on complex geometries produced in a single setup. Geometric tolerances — true position, perpendicularity, flatness, concentricity — are measured and reported against your drawing callout. If a specified tolerance is at the practical limit for the feature or material, DFM review flags it before cutting begins.

**Q2: How do I know the parts actually meet my specifications?**
Every tight-tolerance order ships with a CMM-verified dimensional inspection report. The report records measured value, nominal dimension, tolerance, and pass/fail status for every critical feature — referenced against your drawing revision number, not a generic template. Material certificates accompany every order, tracing the alloy grade and heat number to the specific parts shipped. Weldo CNC holds a 97.48% annual pass rate across all CNC orders, measured at a single in-house facility. Non-conforming parts do not ship.

**Q3: What happens if a part fails inspection?**
Non-conforming parts are quarantined immediately and processed through an NCR — Non-Conformance Report — which documents the deviation, root cause, and disposition decision. Disposition options are rework, replacement, or concession — all require written approval before any action is taken. No failed part ships. You are notified of any disposition that affects your delivery timeline before it becomes a problem, not after.

**Q4: Is Weldo CNC ISO 9001:2015 certified?**
Yes. Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification, audited annually by an accredited third-party body. The certificate covers the full production chain — supplier qualification, incoming material inspection, in-process control, final inspection, non-conformance handling, and shipping release. ISO 9001:2015 is not self-declared: the auditing body verifies conformance to the standard independently. A copy of the certificate is available on request.

**Q5: Does quality inspection add to my lead time?**
Inspection runs in parallel with production — not after it. In-process measurements happen at the machine during cutting. Final CMM inspection is scheduled as part of the production sequence, not bolted on at the end. Prototype parts ship in 1–5 business days. Small-batch orders ship in 5–15 business days. Weldo CNC's 98% on-time delivery rate reflects a production system where quality checks are built into the schedule, not treated as an afterthought.

**Q6: Do you perform first article inspection (FAI)?**
Yes. FAI — First Article Inspection, a full dimensional layout on the first piece of a new production run — is available on all production orders and performed as standard on first runs for new parts. The FAI report establishes the dimensional baseline for every subsequent batch. Process capability data — Cpk, a measure of how consistently the process holds the specified tolerance — is available on request for ongoing production programmes.

**Q7: What is the minimum order quantity for CNC machining at Weldo CNC?**
There is no minimum order quantity. Single-part prototype orders are accepted. Weldo CNC machines parts from 1 piece through to full production volumes. The same quality process — CMM inspection, material certs, documentation — applies whether you order one part or one thousand. Prototype runs are priced accordingly and processed through the same quality management system.

**Q8: What file formats does Weldo CNC need to verify tolerances against my drawing?**
STEP (.stp / .step) files are preferred for 3D geometry. Engineering drawings with tolerance callouts should be supplied as PDF or DWG. Both the 3D model and the 2D drawing are required for tight-tolerance work — CMM inspection references the drawing revision number, not just the model geometry. Additional supported formats include IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), Parasolid (.x_t), and reference images (.jpg / .png). Upload files directly on the quote page.

**Q9: How much does quality documentation cost?**
Dimensional inspection reports and material certificates are included in the part price — not charged as extras. CMM reports are standard on tight-tolerance orders at no additional cost. FAI reports, RoHS declarations, and compliance documentation are available on request for production orders. If you need a document not listed here, confirm the requirement at the quoting stage and it will be factored into the order.

**Q10: How does Weldo CNC's quality process compare to Hubs or Xometry?**
Hubs quotes CNC tolerances to ±0.020 mm as standard. Xometry routes orders through a marketplace network — your part goes to whichever shop has capacity, with no single facility quality standard. Weldo CNC machines every order in-house at its own 100-machine facility in Dongguan, China — one facility, one quality system, CMM-verified report with every tight-tolerance order. The 97.48% annual pass rate is measured across a single operation, not averaged across a supplier network.
