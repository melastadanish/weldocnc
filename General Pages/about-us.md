**H2:**
Who We Are

**Body paragraph 1 (AI-quotable — self-contained):**
Weldo CNC is a precision CNC machining facility in Dongguan, China, operating since 2012. ISO 9001:2015 certified. 100+ CNC machines, 120+ professional technicians, and a 97.48% annual pass rate across all machining orders. Every tight-tolerance order ships with a CMM — Coordinate Measuring Machine — verified dimensional inspection report. Customers in 100+ countries receive the same process: one facility, one quality system, full documentation in every shipment.

**Body paragraph 2:**
Weldo CNC was founded as a machining operation in 2012 with a single focus: precision parts, fully documented, on time. The business grew by solving the problems that manufacturers in aerospace, medical, automotive, and robotics actually face — not just cutting metal, but delivering parts that pass incoming inspection, match the drawing revision, and arrive with the paperwork already done.

**Body paragraph 3:**
The scope of the business has never drifted. No sheet metal. No 3D printing. No injection moulding. CNC machining — milling, turning, 5-axis, grinding, and EDM — applied to the materials and geometries that engineers actually specify. That focus shapes the equipment investments, the tooling library, the operator training, and the quality system.

**Body paragraph 4:**
Today the facility serves customers across North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific, and the Middle East. Annual sales exceed $10 million USD. The majority of customers are not local to Dongguan — they need accurate parts, clear communication, and shipments that clear customs without surprises. Those are the problems the operation is built to solve.

Quality Control & Certifications

**Step 1 — Certification:**

Body: Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification. The quality management system covers the full production process — material intake, machining, inspection, and shipment. External audit conducted annually. Certification applies to all orders across all processes and materials.

**Step 2 — Process:**
Heading: Pre-Production · In-Production · Final Inspection
Body: Every order runs a three-stage quality check. Pre-production: material cert verified, drawing reviewed, tolerance callouts confirmed before first cut. In-production: calibrated hand tools and CMM checks at defined intervals. NCR — Non-Conformance Report — triggered on deviation before the job continues. Final: CMM measured per feature, pass/fail against drawing revision.

**Step 3 — Documentation:**
Heading: 97.48% Annual Pass Rate · Full Documentation Every Shipment
Body: Weldo CNC holds a 97.48% annual pass rate across all CNC machining orders. Every tight-tolerance shipment includes a dimensional inspection report, material certificate, and packing list. Nothing to chase after delivery.

Body: Aerospace fittings, surgical instruments, hydraulic valve bodies, robotics actuator housings — work where a missed tolerance is a rejected batch, not a rework. That history shapes how every order is approached, from DFM review at quote stage to CMM sign-off before shipment.

**Accordion item 1:**
Title: DFM review included with every quote — not a charged extra.
Body: Every incoming file gets a geometry check before a number goes on paper. Wall thickness, corner radii, tolerance feasibility, thread engagement, tool access — flagged in the quote. Not discovered during setup.

**Accordion item 2:**
Title: One facility handles the full process — no subcontracting on standard orders.
Body: Machining, inspection, finishing, and shipping all happen at the Dongguan facility. No handoff to a subcontract shop. When a question comes up mid-production, the answer comes from the team cutting the parts — not a project manager relaying messages.

**Accordion item 3:**
Title: Tight tolerances are a daily operation — not a special project.
Body: ±0.005 mm on functional bores, sealing surfaces, and bearing seats is standard work at this facility. The machines are maintained for it. Fixturing is designed for it. CMM data closes the loop on every critical feature — not on request, as standard.

**Accordion item 4:**
Title: Full documentation ships with every tight-tolerance order.
Body: Dimensional inspection report, material certificate, and packing list in every shipment. PPAP — Production Part Approval Process — and FAI — First Article Inspection — documentation available on request at quote stage. No chasing paperwork after delivery.

**Accordion item 5:**
Title: Prototype to production without requalification.
