## Section 10: [Plastic Name] Machining Design Guidelines
**Design:** H2 + subheading. Full-width 3-column table (Feature | Recommendation | Why It Matters) + 2 supporting paragraphs below. White background.
**H2:** [Plastic Name] Machining Design Guidelines
**Subheading:** Rules specific to machining [plastic name] — plastics behave differently from metals under the same cutting conditions.

Table: minimum 8 rows, maximum 12 rows. Must be specific to this plastic — not generic DFM rules. Include:
- Wall thickness minimum for this plastic
- Internal corner radius
- Tool sharpness requirement (plastics melt rather than chip if tools are dull)
- Coolant/cutting fluid note (some plastics absorb moisture; some need air blast not flood coolant)
- Chip clearance note (plastics produce stringy chips that can re-weld to surface)
- Thread design (self-tapping vs cut threads, helicoil inserts for load-bearing)
- Heat management (many plastics have low melting points — feed rates and spindle speeds need adjusting)
- Moisture / pre-machining conditioning (nylon must be dried; PEEK is stable; acrylic may crack if stressed)
- Tolerance note: plastics have higher thermal expansion coefficients than metals — tight tolerances should account for operating temperature range
- Surface finish: achievable Ra range for this plastic and whether polishing is practical

Supporting paragraphs (2):
- Para 1: The 1–2 most common failures specific to machining this plastic — melting/gumming, stress cracking, dimensional shift post-machining — what causes them and the fix
- Para 2: How plastic-specific issues are caught at DFM review — flagged before cutting, not after
