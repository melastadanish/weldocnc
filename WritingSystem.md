## 1. Brand Voice

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Person | Second person — "your parts", "your project", "your tolerances" |
| Tone | Confident, direct, technically grounded. Never salesy. |
| Claims | Every number traces to `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md`. No unverified claims. |
| Audience | Engineers, buyers, and procurement leads. Use correct technical terms — explain them inline on first use. |
| Lead with | Outcomes — "Parts to ±0.005 mm, delivered in 5 days" not "we have advanced machining capabilities" |
| Paragraph length | Max 3 sentences per paragraph |
| Numbers | Embedded in sentences — never floated as standalone labels or badges |
| Persona | Write as: technical writer with a mechanical engineering background and 10+ years in CNC manufacturing |

1. What does this service/material actually cover?
2. Is this for parts like mine?
3. What tolerances and quality can I expect?

5. What could go wrong?
6. Why WeldoCNC over other suppliers?

## 3. Copy Rules

### Headline Rules
- State what the buyer **gains or achieves** — never what the process **has**
- 5–8 words for section headlines · 3–5 words for card/feature labels
- No adjective without a number behind it — "precise" never appears alone
- Must pass the 3-second test: a reader skimming stops here

### Sentence Rules
- One idea per sentence
- Maximum 20 words per sentence in body copy
- Active voice only — no passive constructions
- "You" appears more than "we" in every section
- Mix lengths: short (5–10 words) for punch · medium (11–20 words) for explanation
- No two consecutive sentences may start with the same word

### CTA Rules
Every CTA states exactly what the reader receives when they click.

**Format:** [Action verb] + [specific thing the reader receives]

✅ "Request a quote for your part geometry and material"
✅ "Download the tolerance reference sheet"
✅ "Compare our aluminium grades side by side"
❌ "Learn more" · "Click here" · "Find out more" · "Explore our services"

One CTA per section maximum.

### Banned Words and Phrases
| Category | Banned |
|---|---|
| Filler adjectives | cutting-edge · state-of-the-art · world-class · next-generation · innovative · revolutionary · game-changing · industry-leading · advanced (alone) · powerful (alone) · high quality · premium (alone) · robust · seamless · seamlessly |
| Corporate verbs | leverage · elevate · empower · revolutionize · transform · optimize · streamline · unlock · harness · pioneer |
| Vague descriptors | reliable (without spec number) · durable (without cycle count or test data) · versatile · flexible · comprehensive · holistic · end-to-end · tailor-made · bespoke |
| Opening clichés | In today's world · In the modern era · In an ever-changing landscape · As technology evolves |
| Soft openers | Whether you're looking for · Look no further · If you're searching for · The good news is |
| False authority | Our team of experts · Dedicated team · Passionate professionals · Years of experience (without a year number) |
| Weak CTAs | Learn more · Click here · Find out more · Discover more · See more · Explore our range |
| Chest-beating | At WeldoCNC, we believe · We are proud to · We are committed to · Our mission is to |
| Manipulation | Peace of mind (replace with specific outcome) · Don't miss out · Act now · Limited time |

## 4. Copywriting Frameworks

### FAB — Feature → Advantage → Benefit
Use for: capability sections, material highlights, why-choose-us cards.

Lead with the Benefit. Feature supports it.

**"So what?" method:** After every sentence, ask "So what?" Keep asking until you reach the real buyer outcome. That outcome leads the sentence.

**"Which means that" bridge:** "[Feature] — which means that [benefit]." Then rewrite benefit-first.

Example:
- Feature: 5-axis simultaneous machining
- Benefit-first: "Complex geometries in one setup — 5-axis simultaneous machining eliminates repositioning error and cuts your lead time."

### PAS — Problem → Agitate → Solution
Use for: FAQ answers, objection handling, technology sections.

- Problem: Repositioning a part between setups introduces cumulative error.
- Agitate: On tight-tolerance parts, that error compounds across every feature.
- Solution: 5-axis simultaneous machining holds all features in one clamping — tolerance stays within ±0.003 mm.

### AIDA — Attention → Interest → Desire → Action
Use for: hero sections, service page introductions.

### StoryBrand
Use for: about sections, homepage narrative.
- Engineer is the hero — they need a part made to spec, fast
- WeldoCNC is the tool — it solves the sourcing problem
- Brand is the guide — knowledgeable, not the star of the story

## 6. SEO Rules

### Keyword Placement — 7 Slots Per Page
| Slot | Location | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | H1 | Exact match — no decoration, no additions |
| 2 | First sentence, opening paragraph | Primary keyword in first 10 words |
| 3 | One mid-page H2 | Exact match or close secondary |
| 4 | One body paragraph | Different section from slot 3 |
| 5 | At least one image alt text | Keyword in natural context |
| 6 | CTA section body copy | Embedded naturally |
| 7 | Meta description | Primary keyword in first 10 words |

- Never use the primary keyword twice in the same paragraph
- Never force it — rewrite the sentence if it reads unnaturally

### Meta Tags
- **Title:** `[Primary Keyword] | WeldoCNC` — max 60 characters
- **Description:** Benefit-led, primary keyword in first 10 words — max 155 characters
- Every page must have a unique title and description

### Internal Links
- Every page links to at least 2 other pages
- Anchor text must use the exact target keyword of the destination page
- Never use "click here", "learn more", or "here" as anchor text
- Follow SILO tier paths — link within correct tier only

### SILO Rules
| Tier | Page type | Link direction |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | Homepage | Links DOWN to all Tier 2 pages |
| Tier 2 | Service overview pages | Links UP to Tier 1, DOWN to Tier 3 |
| Tier 3 | Material / finish / industry sub-pages | Links UP to Tier 2 and Tier 1 only |

No cross-silo links between unrelated service families.

## 7. Quality Check System

### Overview
| Tier | When | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 — Section checks | After each section, before showing user | Catch failures early |
| Tier 2 — Four passes | After full page is compiled | Refine a clean page |

---

### Tier 1 — Five Section Checks

Run on every section immediately after writing. Before showing the user.

#### Check 1 — Banned Phrase Scan
Read word by word against the banned list in Section 3.
**Pass:** Zero matches. **Fail:** Any match → rewrite the full sentence with a real number or real outcome.

#### Check 2 — Claims Verification
For every factual claim:
1. Find the exact number in `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md`. If it is not there, remove it.
2. Every number must reach Level 3 specificity minimum:
```
Level 1 (fails): "Tight tolerances"
Level 2 (fails): "Very tight tolerances"
Level 3 (passes): "Tolerances to ±0.005 mm"
Level 4 (target): "Tolerances to ±0.005 mm — confirmed by CMM report included with every order"
```

#### Check 3 — Consumer Readability
**A — Stranger test:** Read as a buyer who knows nothing about CNC. Every unexplained technical term is a failure. Format: "[Term] — [plain English explanation]"
**B — Sentence length:** Flag any sentence over 25 words. Split at the earliest natural break.
**C — Passive voice:** Find and rewrite all passive constructions.
**D — Jargon ratio:** Every technical term explained inline on first use. Unexplained terms: zero.

#### Check 4 — Benefit-First Test
Every sentence describing a process or capability must open with the buyer outcome, not the product attribute.

**Fail:** "Our 5-axis machines can hold ±0.003 mm"
**Pass:** "Complex features hold to ±0.003 mm — 5-axis simultaneous machining eliminates the repositioning error that degrades tolerance"

#### Check 5 — CTA Specificity Test (CTA sections only)
1. **Substitution test:** Replace CTA with "Click here." If meaning doesn't change, it fails.
2. **Value exchange test:** Does the CTA state what the reader receives? Required: [Action verb] + [specific thing]
3. **Section alignment test:** Does the CTA match what the section was about?

---

### Tier 2 — Four-Pass System

**MANDATORY:** All four passes run together after the full page is compiled. Never show content after Pass A or Pass C alone. Sequence: write full page → Pass A → Pass B → Pass D → Pass C → show final. Do not present intermediate drafts.

#### Pass A — Full-Page Quality Audit
Read the full page once. Mark every sentence that fails any Tier 1 check. Score six dimensions by failure count.

**Scoring:** 0 failures = 10/10 · 1 = 9/10 · 2–3 = 7–8/10 · 4–6 = 5–6/10 · 7–10 = 3–4/10 · 11+ = 1–2/10

**Six dimensions:**
- **Clarity** — unexplained jargon + sentences over 25 words
- **Specificity** — unverified claims + Level 1/2 claims
- **Human Voice** — banned phrases + same-word consecutive starts + sentences that sound assembled from a template
- **Buyer Fit** — feature-first sentences + unexplained industry assumptions
- **Benefit-First** — sentences where feature appears before buyer benefit
- **Originality** — repeated phrases + structural repetition (3+ sentences same pattern)

**The "assembled" test:** Read aloud. If it sounds built from a template — subject → verb → object, three+ sentences in identical structure, vocabulary technically correct but no engineer would say it — it fails Human Voice.

#### Pass B — Surgical Rewrite
Fix every Pass A failure. Only those items. Do not rewrite passing sentences.

**Eight rewriting rules:**
1. **Start variation** — No two consecutive sentences start with the same word.
2. **Number grounding** — Every vague claim gets a specific verified number from `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md`.
3. **Sentence length** — Over 25 words: split. Two ideas joined by "and" or "but": test as two sentences.
4. **Active voice** — Find the agent of the action. Make it the subject.
5. **One idea per sentence** — Comma joining two independent clauses: test whether each is stronger alone.
6. **Sentence rhythm** — Short sentences punch. Long sentences explain. Three+ same length: vary one.
7. **Buyer language** — Use words buyers use. "Machine your part" not "manufacture your component". "Quote" not "RFQ response".
8. **No urgency language** — Engineers research suppliers over days or weeks. Urgency reads as pressure. Remove without replacement.

#### Pass D — GEO & E-E-A-T Check
Run after Pass B. Six binary checks — pass or fail.

| Check | Pass condition |
|---|---|
| AI-quotable paragraph | Present in opening section. Self-contained. Contains brand name, service category, one spec, one differentiator. |
| FAQ coverage | All 8 required topics answered. Each answer 3+ sentences. Each answer spec-anchored. |
| Comparison FAQ | One FAQ directly compares WeldoCNC to a named competitor. Spec-to-spec. No vague claims. |
| Entity clarity block | Brand name, service category, primary use case, and one differentiator all appear within first 100 words. |
| Expertise signals | Correct technical terms used. Every term explained inline on first use. At least one spec with the "why it matters" bridge. |
| Authority signals | At least 2 approved authority statements from `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md` present. No unverified claims. |

Any fail = fix before Pass C.

#### Pass C — Buyer Conversion Review
Read as an engineer comparing 3 suppliers, will leave if not convinced in 90 seconds.

**Six tests:**

**Test 1 — 3-second headline test**
Read only section headlines. Does each make a promise or state an outcome worth reading more about?
Fail: describes the section, names the process, uses adjectives without proof.
Pass: states an outcome, makes a promise, raises a question the next sentence answers.

**Test 2 — First 10 words test**
Read only the first 10 words of each section's body copy. Do they state what the buyer gains?
First 10 words introducing the company, machine type, or process = fail.

**Test 3 — Objection coverage test**
Cross-check full page against this list — every objection must be answered with a spec-backed response:
- Tolerance capability · Lead time · Quality verification · Supplier credibility · Material range · Order volume · File formats · Industry experience

**Test 4 — CTA journey test**
Map every CTA. Each must advance the reader one step closer to a decision. No two CTAs to same destination.
- Early page: orientation (find the right service or material)
- Mid page: verification (download spec sheet, see tolerance table)
- End of page: conversion (request a quote)

**Test 5 — Differentiation test**
At least 2 of 5 differentiators must appear explicitly:
- ISO 9001:2015 with 97.48% annual pass rate
- CMM-verified inspection report with every order
- 100+ CNC machines, in-house facility — no subcontracting
- 1–5 day prototype lead time
- 100+ machinable materials across metals and engineering plastics

**Test 6 — Read-aloud test**
Read the full page aloud. Mark every hesitation, every sentence read twice, every phrase that sounds written not spoken. Every marked sentence is a rewrite.

**AI-Written Score — target 15% or below**

Signals that raise the score (+5% each):
- Three+ consecutive sentences in identical grammatical structure
- "not only... but also" construction
- Transitions: Furthermore · Additionally · Moreover · In conclusion · It is worth noting
- Perfect parallel bullets (every bullet same length and structure)
- Any CTA that could apply to any machining shop anywhere
- Vocabulary technically correct but no engineer would say it

Signals that lower the score (-5% each):
- Intentional sentence fragment for punch
- Sentence starting with "And" or "But"
- A specific non-round number
- Direct mid-paragraph address to the reader
- An unexpected analogy grounding a technical concept
- A rhetorical question answered immediately by the next sentence

If above 15%: identify top 3 AI-signal sentences, rewrite, reassess. Maximum 2 loops.

## 8. GEO / AI SEO Rules

> These rules make WeldoCNC content citable by AI search engines — Perplexity, ChatGPT, Gemini, Google AI Overviews. AI pulls direct, specific, self-contained answers. Content that hedges or vagues out gets skipped.

### The AI-Quotable Paragraph
Every service and material page must contain one paragraph that an AI engine could lift verbatim and it would make complete sense with zero surrounding context.

**Structure:**
```
[Brand] [service/material] [specific capability] — [key spec] — [use case] — [differentiator].
```

**Example (passing):**
> WeldoCNC machines aluminium 6061 to tolerances of ±0.005 mm with a surface finish of Ra 1.6 µm as standard. Prototype parts ship in 1–5 days. ISO 9001:2015 certified — every order includes a CMM-verified dimensional inspection report.

**Example (failing):**
> Our aluminium machining services are designed for precision and speed. We work with a range of grades to meet your requirements.

Place in the opening section of every service and material page. It doubles as the schema description field.

### FAQ Standards — Mandatory Rules

FAQs are the highest GEO-value section on any page.

**Minimum per FAQ answer:**
- Length: 3–5 sentences minimum
- Self-contained: makes sense with no surrounding context
- Spec-anchored: contains at least one verified number from `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md`
- PAS structure preferred

**Question format:**
- Write exactly as a person would type into ChatGPT or Perplexity
- Full sentence questions: "What tolerance can WeldoCNC hold on aluminium parts?" not "Aluminium tolerance"
- Include the material or process in the question where it aids AI matching

**Required FAQ topics — every service/material page must cover all eight:**
| Topic | Example question form |
|---|---|
| Tolerance / precision | "What tolerance can you hold on [material/process]?" |
| Lead time | "How long does it take to machine [material/part type]?" |
| Quality verification | "How do I know the parts meet my specs?" |
| Order volume | "What is the minimum order quantity for CNC machining?" |
| File formats | "What file formats do you accept for CNC quotes?" |
| Material options | "What grades of [material] do you machine?" |
| Cost / pricing | "How much does CNC machining cost for [material/part]?" |
| Comparison | "How does WeldoCNC compare to [competitor]?" |

### Entity Clarity Block
Every page must establish four signals within the first 100 words:

| Signal | Example |
|---|---|
| Brand name | "WeldoCNC" — not "we" or "our team" |
| Service/material category | "aluminium CNC machining" — exact keyword match |
| Primary use case | "prototype and production parts for aerospace and medical applications" |
| Key differentiator | "ISO 9001:2015 certified, CMM-verified" or "1–5 day prototype delivery" |

### Structured Answer Patterns
| Intent | Opener pattern |
|---|---|
| Capability | "[Service/material] machines to / holds / produces..." |
| Quality | "Every order includes [specific deliverable]" |
| Comparison | "Unlike [competitor], WeldoCNC [specific difference]" |
| How-it-works | "When [trigger], the [process] [specific action]" |
| Spec confirmation | "[Spec] — confirmed on [process] — [context]" |

Avoid: "It's important to note that..." · "There are several factors..." · "This depends on..."

## 9. E-E-A-T Standards

### Experience Signals
Every service/material page must include at least two:

| Signal type | Implementation |
|---|---|
| Real use case scenario | "A titanium Grade 5 bracket for an aerospace assembly — 5-axis machining holds the ±0.003 mm position tolerance across all six mounting holes" |
| Manufacturing context | "Ships complete — part, CMM report, and packing documentation in one shipment. Nothing chased separately." |
| Failure mode addressed | "Most tolerance failures happen at part transfer between setups. 5-axis simultaneous machining eliminates that transfer — all critical features cut in one clamping." |
| Material expertise | "Aluminium 7075 work-hardens faster than 6061 under aggressive cuts. Feed rates and coolant strategy are adjusted per grade — not treated as the same material." |

Do not fabricate experience signals. Every scenario must be grounded in confirmed capability from `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md`.

### Expertise Signals
**Format:**
```
[Technical term] — [plain English] — [why it matters to the buyer]
```

> CMM inspection — coordinate measuring machine, measures part geometry to micron accuracy — which means every critical dimension on your drawing is verified against an actual number, not estimated by eye.

**Required technical terms by page type:**
| Page type | Required terms |
|---|---|
| Milling pages | End mill, feed rate, depth of cut, fixturing |
| Turning pages | Chuck, tool nose radius, runout, G-code |
| 5-axis pages | Simultaneous 5-axis, trunnion, undercut, single-setup |
| Material pages | Machinability rating, work hardening, chip control, surface finish (Ra) |
| Surface finish pages | Ra value, grit sequence, adhesion, masking |

### Authoritativeness Signals
**Mandatory authority statements — use these verbatim:**

| Claim | Approved copy | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Certification | "ISO 9001:2015 certified" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |
| Pass rate | "97.48% annual pass rate" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |
| On-time delivery | "98% on-time delivery" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |
| Scale | "100+ CNC machines in-house" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |
| Team | "120+ professional technicians" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |
| Reach | "Customers in 100+ countries" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |
| Founded | "Operating since 2012" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |
| Materials | "100+ machinable materials" | CLIENT-DATA-MAP |

Do not add authority claims beyond this list.

### Trustworthiness Signals
- Every spec number links to `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md` — if not there, remove it
- No testimonials, star ratings, or review counts unless real data supplied by client
- No "industry-leading", "most trusted", "top-rated" without a verifiable source
- No fabricated project counts or installation numbers

**Trust-negative patterns — remove on sight:**
- "Trusted by thousands of engineers" — unverified
- "Award-winning precision" — no award confirmed
- "Guaranteed satisfaction" — not a confirmed policy
- Any claim not traceable to `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md`

|---|---|---|
| Feature lead | "Our machines use 5-axis simultaneous technology" | "Complex geometries machined in one setup — 5-axis eliminates repositioning error" |
| Vague tolerance claim | "Tight tolerances" | "Tolerances to ±0.005 mm" |

| Generic CTA | "Contact us to learn more" | "Request a quote for your part — upload your STEP file and receive a response in 24 hours" |
| Chest-beating opener | "At WeldoCNC, we pride ourselves on precision" | Lead with what the buyer gets, not what we feel |
| Passive quality claim | "Parts are inspected before shipping" | "Every order ships with a full CMM-verified dimensional inspection report" |

## 11. What NOT to Do

- Never invent specs. Every number from `CLIENT-DATA-MAP.md`.
- Never claim certifications beyond ISO 9001:2015 unless client confirms additional certs.
- Never reference non-CNC services (3D printing, injection molding, welding) on the new site.
- Never write "We offer..." — second person or third person only.
- Never run four-pass system on individual sections — Tier 1 per section, four passes on full compiled page only.
- Never float spec numbers as standalone labels — embed in sentences.
- Never skip the buyer journey read before passes.
- Never write two CTAs in the same section.
- Never use urgency language — engineers research suppliers over days or weeks.
- Never present a section to the user before running all five Tier 1 checks.
