**H2:** Why Engineers Look for an Xometry Alternative

**Card 1 — Marketplace Quality Variability**
Xometry's AI Quoting Engine® matches orders to whichever supplier in its network has capacity and meets the quoted capability on paper. The supplier who machines your part this month may not be the supplier who machines it next month. Quality, documentation standards, tooling approaches, and communication style vary across thousands of network partners. For tight-tolerance or documentation-critical programmes, that variability is a structural risk that the platform's supplier vetting cannot fully eliminate.

**Card 2 — Tolerance Ceiling at ±0.025 mm**
Xometry states sub ±0.001″ (approximately ±0.025 mm) as their CNC headline spec. Weldo CNC holds ±0.005 mm as in-house standard — 5× tighter — verified by CMM on every critical-tolerance order. For precision parts where ±0.025 mm is the floor rather than the target, a dedicated CNC facility with a confirmed and traceable tolerance spec is the stronger choice.

**Card 3 — Marketplace Margin and Multi-Process Scope**
Xometry's model spans CNC machining, 3D printing, sheet metal, injection molding, and die casting. That breadth serves buyers who want a single platform across processes. For engineers whose requirement is CNC machining specifically — and who want the tightest tolerance, deepest material knowledge, and direct engineer contact — a CNC-specialist direct manufacturer offers a structurally different proposition: no platform margin, no scope dilution, and the machinist who quoted is the machinist who cuts.

**H2:** Weldo CNC vs Xometry — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerance can Weldo CNC hold compared to Xometry?**
Weldo CNC holds ±0.005 mm as in-house standard for CNC milling and turning — approximately 5× tighter than Xometry's stated sub ±0.025 mm specification. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. All critical-tolerance features are CMM-verified — coordinate measuring machine — and reported per feature on the dimensional inspection report that ships with every tight-tolerance order. Xometry's tolerance is network-dependent: it reflects the best capability available across their supplier base, not a single facility's confirmed spec.

**Q2: What CNC machining services does Weldo CNC offer?**
CNC milling (3-axis to 5-axis), CNC turning, 5-axis CNC machining, precision grinding, wire EDM, and 50+ surface finishing options — anodizing, passivation, powder coating, nickel plating, electropolishing, and more. All services are in-house at the Dongguan facility. No subcontracting on standard orders.

**Q3: What materials can Weldo CNC machine?**
100+ metals and engineering plastics. Metals include aluminium (6061, 7075, 2024, MIC-6), stainless steel (303, 304, 316L, 17-4PH), titanium (Grade 2, Grade 5), brass, copper, carbon steel, alloy steel, and tool steel. Engineering plastics include PEEK, Delrin (POM), Nylon (PA), Polycarbonate, ABS, PTFE, and Acrylic. Confirm your specific grade at quote stage.

**Q4: What are the lead times?**
Prototype parts (simple geometry): 1–5 business days manufacturing. Small batch production: 5–15 business days. Full production runs: 2–5 weeks. Transit to UK, Europe, and North America: 3–7 days on top of manufacturing time. Total lead time confirmed at quote — not estimated after the order is placed.

**Q5: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One part to production quantities — the same quality process applies regardless of volume.

**Q6: What file formats do you accept?**
STEP is preferred — it carries the geometry and tolerances cleanly for quoting and programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). Include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file for any precision features.

**Q7: How does Weldo CNC pricing compare to Xometry?**
For a single simple part at low tolerance, Xometry's instant quoting can be competitive — the AI engine price-shops across its network. For tight-tolerance work (±0.005–0.025 mm), full documentation requirements (CMM report, material cert), or quantities of 10–500 parts, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower — no marketplace margin, no supplier allocation overhead. Upload your STEP file and compare the quote directly.

**Q8: What is the core difference between Weldo CNC and Xometry?**
Manufacturing model. Xometry is an AI marketplace — your order goes to one of thousands of network suppliers, selected by algorithm based on capacity and capability claims. Weldo CNC is a direct manufacturer — 100+ CNC machines in-house, single facility in Dongguan, one quality system. The CMM report and material certificate that ship with every Weldo CNC order are not supplier-dependent outputs — they are standard products of the same in-house process on every job. When consistency across a production run matters, or when documentation is an acceptance condition, a direct manufacturer removes the variability that a marketplace cannot.

**Q9: Xometry holds AS9100D, ISO 13485, and IATF 16949 certifications — does Weldo CNC match that?**
Weldo CNC is ISO 9001:2015 certified. AS9100D, ISO 13485, and IATF 16949 are not currently held. Xometry's platform-level certifications cover their own operations and quality management — whether the specific supplier who machines your part holds those certifications varies by supplier. For programmes that contractually require AS9100D or ISO 13485 at sub-supplier level, confirm the certification requirement at quoting. For development, prototype, and pre-production work where ISO 9001:2015 with rigorous CMM inspection meets the programme requirement, Weldo CNC is a strong and typically faster option.

**Q10: Xometry is U.S.-based — how does Weldo CNC serve U.S. engineers?**
DDP shipping — Delivered Duty Paid — to the USA. Weldo CNC pays the applicable Section 301 tariffs, standard import duty, and handles customs clearance before parts reach the U.S. destination address. Transit time: 3–7 business days via DHL Express or FedEx International Priority. Parts arrive at your U.S. address with no import charges and no customs broker required. For U.S. engineers comparing on total landed cost, include the tariff and duty in the Xometry comparison — under DDP, Weldo CNC's quoted price is the delivered price.
