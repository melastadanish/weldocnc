## Section 2: Why Engineers Look for a RapidDirect Alternative
**Design:** 3-column reason cards. Light grey background.
**Heading:** Why Engineers Look for a RapidDirect Alternative

**Card 1 — Platform vs Engineering Contact**
RapidDirect operates as an online platform with automated quoting. Complex parts — tight positional tolerances, 5-axis geometry, unusual material grades — benefit from an engineer reviewing the drawing before quoting, not an automated system generating a number. DFM issues caught at quoting save time and money; caught after manufacturing, they cost both.

**Card 2 — Documentation Consistency**
For programmes requiring CMM dimensional reports, material heat traceability, and finish process records as a baseline — not an optional extra — the documentation standard needs to be consistent across every order, not variable by job type or quantity tier. If documentation is critical to your QMS or customer requirements, it needs to be guaranteed, not requested.

**Card 3 — Tolerance Depth on Complex Geometry**
Standard tolerance machining is a commodity. ±0.005mm on complex 5-axis geometry, with full CMM verification of every critical dimension, is a more specific capability. If your parts are operating at the tighter end of the tolerance band, the difference in precision between a general-purpose online manufacturer and a facility set up for that work becomes measurable.

## Section 6: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background.
**Heading:** Weldo CNC vs RapidDirect — FAQs

**Q1: Both are Chinese manufacturers — what's the real difference?**
Manufacturing location is the same. The differences are in quoting process (automated vs engineer-reviewed), tolerance depth on complex geometry, and documentation standard. If your parts are straightforward and your tolerance band is generous, the difference is small. If you're working to ±0.005–0.01mm on 5-axis geometry with full documentation requirements, the process matters.

**Q2: How does DFM review work at Weldo CNC?**
An engineer reviews your STEP file and drawing before we quote. We check for features that are technically achievable but could be optimised for manufacturability, tolerances that are tighter than the geometry requires, and any aspect of the design that is likely to affect cost, lead time, or quality. This is included in every quote — not a premium service tier.

**Q3: Is Weldo CNC's documentation standard better than RapidDirect?**
CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and finish process record are standard with every Weldo CNC order. Verify what RapidDirect includes per order — documentation requirements vary by platform tier and job type. For programmes where complete traceability is non-negotiable, confirm explicitly what arrives with your parts before ordering from any supplier.

**Q4: Can Weldo CNC handle the same processes as RapidDirect?**
CNC milling, turning, 5-axis machining, and surface finishing — yes. RapidDirect also offers injection moulding, sheet metal, and 3D printing. Weldo CNC focuses on CNC machining. If your programme requires multiple processes, assess which supplier handles the machined components better and source other processes separately.

**Q5: How long does delivery take?**
Manufacturing from 3 business days for standard aluminium parts. DDP transit: UK and Europe 3–5 days, USA 3–5 days. Total from order confirmation: 6–10 business days for most jobs.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One part to production quantities.
