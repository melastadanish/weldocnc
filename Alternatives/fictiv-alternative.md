## Section 2: Why Engineers Look for a Fictiv Alternative
**Design:** 3-column reason cards. Light grey background.
**Heading:** Why Engineers Look for a Fictiv Alternative

**Card 1 — Platform Pricing at Volume**
Fictiv positions itself as a premium platform — and prices accordingly. For a single prototype where speed and DFM support justify the cost, the model works. For 20, 50, or 200 parts where per-unit cost becomes a line item on the programme budget, the platform margin makes Fictiv difficult to sustain as a regular supplier.

**Card 2 — Network Manufacturing Model**
Like other marketplace platforms, Fictiv routes orders to their manufacturing partner network. Quality and documentation consistency depend on which facility handles your job. The platform provides oversight, but the manufacturing is distributed — and so is the variability.

**Card 3 — Material and Geometry Depth**
Fictiv handles standard CNC geometry and common materials well. For specialist alloys — 17-4PH, Inconel, duplex stainless, Grade 5 titanium — or genuinely complex 5-axis geometry with tight positional tolerances, the network model introduces uncertainty about which shop has the actual capability for the specific job.
