**H2:** Why Engineers Look for a Fictiv Alternative

**Card 1 — Network Manufacturing Model**
Fictiv is a sourcing platform, not a manufacturer. When you place a CNC order through Fictiv, it is fulfilled by a manufacturing partner in their global network — facilities in the U.S., Mexico, India, and China. Fictiv provides oversight, DFM review, and programme management, but the machining is distributed across partner shops. Quality, documentation practices, and communication style vary by facility. For tight-tolerance or documentation-critical work, that variability is a risk the platform interface cannot eliminate.

**Card 2 — No Published Tolerance Specification**
Fictiv does not publish a stated CNC tolerance on its homepage — tolerance capability is network-dependent, varying by which partner facility handles the order. Weldo CNC holds ±0.005 mm as a stated, in-house standard — verified by CMM on every critical-tolerance order. For engineers who need a tolerance confirmed before placing an order, a manufacturer with a published spec removes the ambiguity.

**Card 3 — Platform Pricing at Volume**
Fictiv's platform pricing includes a margin layer that does not compress significantly at 20–200 parts. For a single prototype where the platform's DFM support and programme management justify the cost, the model works. For bridge builds and pre-production quantities where per-unit cost becomes a programme budget line item, the platform margin makes Fictiv difficult to sustain as a regular high-volume supplier.

Material verified against mill certificate before any job starts. Drawing reviewed for DFM issues, tolerance feasibility, and finish interactions. **Final-Production:**

**H2:** Weldo CNC vs Fictiv — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerance can Weldo CNC hold on CNC machined parts?**
±0.005 mm is the confirmed standard for CNC milling and turning at Weldo CNC. For 5-axis simultaneous machining on complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. All critical-tolerance features are verified by CMM — coordinate measuring machine — and reported per feature on the dimensional inspection report that ships with every tight-tolerance order. Fictiv does not publish a stated tolerance specification — their capability is network-dependent, varying by which manufacturing partner receives the job.

**Q2: What CNC machining services does Weldo CNC offer?**
CNC milling (3-axis to 5-axis), CNC turning, 5-axis CNC machining, precision grinding, wire EDM, and 50+ surface finishing options — anodizing, passivation, powder coating, nickel plating, electropolishing, and more. All services are in-house at the Dongguan facility. No subcontracting on standard orders.

**Q3: What materials can Weldo CNC machine?**
100+ metals and engineering plastics. Metals include aluminium (6061, 7075, 2024, MIC-6), stainless steel (303, 304, 316L, 17-4PH), titanium (Grade 2, Grade 5), brass, copper, carbon steel, alloy steel, and tool steel. Engineering plastics include PEEK, Delrin (POM), Nylon (PA), Polycarbonate, ABS, PTFE, and Acrylic. Confirm your specific grade at quote stage.

**Q4: What are the lead times?**
Prototype parts (simple geometry): 1–5 business days manufacturing. Small batch production: 5–15 business days. Full production runs: 2–5 weeks. Transit to UK, Europe, and North America: 3–7 days on top of manufacturing time. Total lead time confirmed at quote — not estimated after the order is placed.

**Q5: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One part to production quantities — the same quality process applies regardless of volume.

**Q6: What file formats do you accept?**
STEP is preferred — it carries the geometry and tolerances cleanly for quoting and programming. Also accepted: IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). Include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file for any precision features.

**Q7: How does Weldo CNC pricing compare to Fictiv?**
For a single prototype where Fictiv's programme management and instant quoting add value, the platform pricing is defensible. From 10–20 parts upward, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower — there is no platform margin, and setup cost amortises across units. For pre-production quantities of 50–200 parts with full documentation requirements, the difference is significant. Upload your STEP file and compare the quote directly.

**Q8: What is the core difference between Weldo CNC and Fictiv?**
Manufacturing model. Fictiv is a sourcing platform — it connects buyers to a global network of manufacturing partners. Weldo CNC is a direct manufacturer — 100+ CNC machines in-house, one facility in Dongguan, one quality system. The CMM report and material certificate that ship with every Weldo CNC order are not platform-managed outputs — they are standard products of the same in-house process on every job. When quality or documentation matters, the difference between a platform and a manufacturer is the difference between oversight and accountability.

**Q9: Fictiv offers engineer DFM review and programme managers — does Weldo CNC match that?**
Yes on DFM review — engineer DFM feedback is included with every Weldo CNC quote, no charge, engineer to engineer. The difference is structural: Weldo CNC's DFM reviewer is accountable for the machined outcome, not just the platform's quality score. On programme management, Weldo CNC operates as a direct point of contact — no programme manager layer, but also no platform interface between the engineer and the machinist. For complex multi-part or multi-revision programmes, discuss requirements at quote stage.

**Q10: How does international delivery work — does Weldo CNC handle the same countries as Fictiv?**
Weldo CNC ships DDP — Delivered Duty Paid — to the UK, USA, Germany, France, and most of Europe and North America. DDP means Weldo CNC files the customs declaration, pays the import duty, and handles clearance before parts reach the destination address. Fictiv's logistics is primarily U.S.-focused. For European engineers who found Fictiv's international shipping complex or expensive, Weldo CNC's DDP model removes customs administration entirely — parts arrive at your address with no import charges and no broker required.
