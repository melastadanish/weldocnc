**H2:** Quality Control & Production Inspection

**Pre-Production:**
Material verified against mill certificate before any job starts. Drawing reviewed for DFM issues, tolerance feasibility, and finish interactions. Unlike network-model platforms, every order runs the same quality process at the same facility.

**In-Production:**
In-process checks at defined intervals using calibrated hand tools and CMM. Datum references verified after each setup change. Non-Conformance Reports — NCRs — trigger a production stop before the next operation, not at final inspection.

**Final-Production:**
CMM inspection against drawing revision. Measured values recorded per feature — nominal, tolerance, actual, pass/fail. Material cert, dimensional report, and packing list ship with every order. Documentation is not shop-dependent — it is standard.

**H2:** Weldo CNC vs Hubs — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What's the main difference between Weldo CNC and Hubs?**
Manufacturing model. Hubs is a platform that combines in-house Protolabs factories with a global partner network — your order may go to an in-house facility or to a third-party shop. Weldo CNC machines every order in-house at our own 100+ machine facility in Dongguan. One facility, one quality system, one point of contact. That difference determines quality consistency, documentation reliability, and who is accountable when something needs resolving.

**Q2: What CNC machining services does Weldo CNC offer?**
CNC milling (3-axis to 5-axis), CNC turning, 5-axis CNC machining, precision grinding, wire EDM, and 50+ surface finishing options — anodizing, passivation, powder coating, nickel plating, electropolishing, and more. All services are in-house. No subcontracting.

**Q3: What materials can Weldo CNC machine?**
100+ metals and engineering plastics. Metals include aluminium (6061, 7075, 2024, MIC-6), stainless steel (303, 304, 316L, 17-4PH), titanium (Grade 2, Grade 5), brass, copper, carbon steel, alloy steel, and tool steel. Plastics include PEEK, Delrin (POM), Nylon (PA), Polycarbonate, ABS, PTFE, and Acrylic. If the material is not listed, ask at quote stage.

**Q4: What are the lead times?**
Prototype parts (simple geometry): 1–5 business days manufacturing. Small batch production: 5–15 business days. Full production runs: 2–5 weeks. Transit to UK, Europe, and North America: 3–7 days on top of manufacturing. Total lead time confirmed at quote — not estimated after the order is placed.

**Q5: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One part to production quantities — the same quality process applies regardless of volume.

**Q6: What file formats do you accept?**
STEP is preferred — it carries the geometry and tolerances cleanly. We also accept IGES, SolidWorks (.sldprt), PDF 2D drawings, DXF, and compressed packages (.zip, .rar). Include a 2D drawing with tolerance callouts alongside the STEP file for any precision features.

**Q7: How does Weldo CNC pricing compare to Hubs?**
For simple low-tolerance parts at low quantities, platform pricing can be competitive. For tight-tolerance work (±0.005 mm), full documentation requirements, or batches of 10–500 parts, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or lower — without the platform margin applied to each order. Upload your STEP file and compare the quote directly.

**Q8: Can Weldo CNC guarantee CMM reports and material certificates — unlike Hubs?**
Yes. CMM dimensional inspection report and material certificate are standard with every Weldo CNC order. They are not dependent on which shop receives the job — because there is only one shop. For aerospace, medical, and defence programmes where documentation is an acceptance condition, this is the structural difference that matters.

**Q9: Hubs states ±0.020 mm tolerances. Can Weldo CNC hold tighter?**
Yes. Weldo CNC holds ±0.005 mm as standard — 4× tighter than Hubs' stated headline spec. For 5-axis complex geometry, ±0.003 mm. All critical-tolerance features are CMM-verified and reported per feature on the dimensional inspection report. For features requiring tighter than ±0.005 mm, discuss the specific geometry at quote stage.

**Q10: How does international delivery work — do I need to handle customs?**
No. Weldo CNC ships DDP — Delivered Duty Paid — to the UK, USA, Germany, France, and most of Europe and North America. DDP means Weldo CNC files the customs declaration, pays the import duty, and handles clearance before parts reach the destination. Parts arrive at your address. No customs broker required, no unexpected charges on arrival.
