## Section 2: Why Engineers Look for a Hubs Alternative
**Design:** 3-column reason cards. Light grey background.
**Heading:** Why Engineers Look for a Hubs Alternative

**Card 1 — Network Quality Variation**
Hubs is a manufacturing marketplace. Orders are distributed to shops in their partner network. Quality, communication, and documentation standards vary by shop — and you have limited visibility into which facility is handling your job. For tight-tolerance or documentation-critical work, this is a meaningful risk.

**Card 2 — Documentation Gaps**
Hubs' documentation standard is determined by whichever shop receives the order. CMM reports and material certificates are not guaranteed across the network. For programmes where these are non-negotiable — aerospace tier 2, medical device components, defence procurement — chasing documents after delivery is a recurring problem.

**Card 3 — Tolerance Consistency**
The tolerance capability advertised on the Hubs platform reflects best-case network capability, not a guaranteed standard. ±0.025mm on a simple 3-axis part is achievable across the network. ±0.005mm on a complex 5-axis assembly is a different matter — the individual shop capability determines what you actually receive.

## Section 6: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background.
**Heading:** Weldo CNC vs Hubs — FAQs

**Q1: What's the main difference between Weldo CNC and Hubs?**
Manufacturing model. Hubs is a marketplace — your order goes to a third-party shop in their network. Weldo CNC is a direct manufacturer — we machine your parts in-house. That difference determines quality consistency, documentation reliability, and who you're actually talking to when something needs resolving.

**Q2: Can I get consistent documentation from Weldo CNC?**
Yes — CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and finish process record with every order. This is not dependent on which shop picks up the job, because there is only one facility.

**Q3: How does Weldo CNC handle tight tolerances?**
±0.005mm standard tolerance capability. Complex 5-axis geometry is in-house. For tolerances tighter than ±0.005mm, discuss the specific feature at quoting — we can advise on feasibility and fixturing approach.

**Q4: How does international delivery work?**
DDP to UK, USA, Germany, France, and most of Europe and North America. We file the customs declaration, pay the import duty, and handle clearance. Parts arrive at your door — no customs broker, no unexpected charges.

**Q5: Is Weldo CNC more expensive than Hubs?**
For simple low-tolerance parts at low quantities, marketplace pricing can undercut direct manufacturers. For tight-tolerance work, full documentation requirements, or quantities of 10–500 parts, Weldo CNC is typically competitive or cheaper — without the quality lottery.
