## Section 2: Why Engineers Look for a Protolabs Alternative
**Design:** 3-column reason cards. Light grey background.
**Heading:** Why Engineers Look for a Protolabs Alternative

**Card 1 — Per-Unit Cost at Volume**
Protolabs pricing is structured for rapid single-unit prototyping. At 20, 50, or 100 parts, the per-unit cost remains high — the platform model and US/EU manufacturing base don't offer the cost reduction that comes with volume. Engineers running pre-production or bridge quantities find the economics difficult.

**Card 2 — Geometry and Tolerance Constraints**
Protolabs' instant-quote system is automated. Complex 5-axis geometry, deep pockets, tight positional tolerances, and parts requiring engineering judgement on fixturing often get rejected by the system or quoted at a significant premium. The platform works best within a defined geometry envelope.

**Card 3 — Documentation Depth**
For programmes requiring CMM reports, material traceability, and finish process records as standard — not as optional add-ons — Protolabs' offering can be thin. Their documentation standard is adequate for many prototype applications but not always sufficient for pre-production qualification or regulated industries.

## Section 6: FAQ
**Design:** Accordion. White background.
**Heading:** Weldo CNC vs Protolabs — FAQs

**Q1: Is Weldo CNC faster than Protolabs?**
Protolabs is faster for simple, low-tolerance aluminium parts — their 1–3 day service is difficult to match for standard geometry. For tight-tolerance or complex 5-axis work, the comparison changes: Weldo CNC manufacturing from 3 business days, plus 3–5 days DDP transit. If you need something in 24 hours, a local shop or Protolabs is the honest answer. If you need it in 8–12 days with tight tolerances and full documentation, Weldo CNC is typically the better choice.

**Q2: Can Weldo CNC machine parts that Protolabs rejects?**
Frequently. Protolabs' automated system refuses or heavily prices complex geometry, tight positional tolerances, deep pockets, and parts requiring non-standard fixturing. Send us the drawing — if it's machinable, we'll quote it.

**Q3: How does pricing compare at higher quantities?**
At 1–5 parts, Protolabs can be competitive for standard geometry. At 20–500 parts, Weldo CNC pricing drops significantly — setup cost is spread across units and direct manufacturing eliminates the platform margin. For pre-production and bridge quantities, the difference is substantial.

**Q4: Does Weldo CNC provide documentation to the same standard?**
Better. CMM dimensional report, material certificate, and finish process record are included with every order as standard. Protolabs offers documentation as optional add-ons depending on the service tier.

**Q5: What industries does Weldo CNC serve?**
Aerospace, medical, automotive, defence, robotics, electronics, energy — any industry where tight tolerances, material traceability, and documented quality are the baseline expectation.

**Q6: Is there a minimum order quantity?**
No minimum. One part to production quantities.
