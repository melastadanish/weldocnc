## Section 3: Corrosion, Pressure, and Material Traceability — the Non-Negotiables in Energy CNC Machining
**Design:** 2-column — text left, image right. White background.

**AI-quotable paragraph:**
Weldo CNC machines precision components for the energy industry — valve bodies and trim, pump housings and impellers, manifold assemblies, choke components, turbine hardware, and subsea structural parts — at its ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China, operating since 2012. Tolerances to ±0.005 mm. Materials include 316L and duplex 2205 stainless, super-duplex 2507, Inconel 625 and 718, 17-4PH stainless, titanium Grade 5, and 4140 API-grade chromoly steel. CMM-verified dimensional inspection report and material certificate — including heat number and full chemical composition — ship as standard with every tight-tolerance order. Quote within 24 hours.

Energy industry precision machining operates at the intersection of aggressive corrosive environments, high operating pressures, and zero-tolerance for component failure. Subsea valves and choke components on an oil and gas production tree operate at pressure ratings that make a dimensional non-conformance a safety consequence. A pump handling sour gas condensate in an offshore production environment requires materials with confirmed resistance to hydrogen sulphide — NACE MR0175 compliance is the documentation standard, not a preference. An offshore wind turbine hub that sees combined bending and torsional loads from wind gusts and wave motion must be machined from verified material to dimensional accuracy that ensures bearing fits and structural connections are within specification for the designed service life.

Material selection in the energy sector is driven by the combination of the process fluid, the operating temperature and pressure, and the regulatory framework. Duplex and super-duplex stainless are the primary pump and valve body materials for seawater and production fluid environments — their combination of elevated strength (yield strength approximately 450 MPa for duplex 2205, 550 MPa for super-duplex 2507) and chloride pitting resistance makes them the standard specification across the North Sea, Gulf of Mexico, and other major production regions. Inconel 625 and 718 are specified for the highest-temperature and highest-corrosivity applications — downhole tool components, high-temperature valve trim, heat exchanger components in aggressive service. 17-4PH stainless provides high strength with adequate corrosion resistance for precision components where fatigue loading accompanies corrosive exposure.

Renewable energy machining — offshore and onshore wind, tidal, and concentrated solar — draws on much of the same material and documentation framework. Offshore wind tower and hub components require the same material traceability and CMM documentation that oil and gas specifies. The difference is scale: wind turbine main shaft and hub geometry involves large, complex machined features where 5-axis capability and large-format machining envelope determine what can be produced in a single setup.

## Section 6: Material Trade-off Cards
**Design:** 3-column card grid. Light grey background.
**Heading:** Getting the Material Right on Every Component

**When Duplex 2205 Is Right**
Duplex 2205 is the standard specification for pump and valve bodies in offshore and subsea production environments because it delivers the combination of mechanical strength and chloride corrosion resistance that neither 316L nor standard austenitic stainless can match. Yield strength of approximately 450 MPa — more than double 316L's 170 MPa — allows thinner-wall construction for equivalent pressure ratings, reducing weight and material cost on large valve and pump bodies. Pitting resistance equivalent number (PREN) of approximately 35 confirms chloride resistance adequate for North Sea seawater and most production fluid environments. Duplex machines more aggressively than 316L — it work-hardens rapidly and requires sharp tooling, higher cutting forces, and controlled feeds — but the material is well-characterised and established in energy sector supply chains. For pump and valve applications in production fluid environments with seawater injection or chloride-bearing produced water, duplex 2205 is the engineering-correct standard specification.

**When Super-Duplex 2507 Makes Sense**
Super-duplex 2507 is specified when the operating environment is beyond the reliable service range of duplex 2205 — typically environments with higher chloride concentration, elevated temperature, or H₂S presence that creates combined pitting and stress corrosion cracking risk. PREN for 2507 is approximately 43, providing corrosion resistance adequate for the most aggressive seawater and sour service conditions. Yield strength of approximately 550 MPa allows further wall thickness reduction relative to 2205 for the same pressure rating. The trade-offs: material cost significantly higher than 2205, machining more demanding (more aggressive work-hardening, more heat at the tool), and availability of certified material in the required sizes and heat is more constrained. For projects where 2205 has previously failed through localised corrosion or stress corrosion cracking, super-duplex is the materials engineering answer.

**When Inconel Is Right**
Inconel 625 and 718 are specified when corrosivity, operating temperature, or both exceed the capability of duplex and super-duplex stainless. Inconel 625 provides corrosion resistance in strongly acidic, high-chloride, and mixed sour/chloride environments that stainless alloys fail in — including concentrated acids, high H₂S at elevated temperature, and reducing environments. Inconel 718 adds high-temperature strength (retaining useful mechanical properties to approximately 700°C) for turbine, compressor, and hot gas path applications. The trade-offs are substantial: Inconel is significantly more expensive than duplex stainless in material and machining cost, harder to machine (low thermal conductivity means heat builds at the cutting zone, requiring through-coolant, sharp tooling, and conservative feeds), and requires careful material sourcing to ensure certification against AMS or equivalent specification. For applications where duplex has failed or where the temperature and chemistry place the component outside duplex's reliable service range, Inconel is the engineering answer.

## Section 7: Processes / Services
**Design:** 4-column service card grid. Light grey background.
**Heading:** Manufacturing Capabilities for Energy Applications
**Cards:** [[cnc-milling-services]] | [[cnc-turning-services]] | [[5-axis-cnc-machining-services]] | [[surface-finishing-services]]

Energy valve bodies and pump casings are typically large, heavy components with multiple bored features, flanged connections, and internal fluid-path geometry that require machining from several approach angles. CNC turning handles large pump casing bores, valve body ID geometry, and flange face turning. Multi-axis turning-milling centres machine cross-drilled ports, off-axis flange holes, and actuator mount features. 5-axis milling handles complex valve body profiles and impeller geometry where the hydraulic fluid path must be machined from multiple approach angles to produce the correct profile without datum transfer error. For components with tight bore concentricity requirements — pump casings where impeller clearance determines hydraulic efficiency, valve bodies where seat-to-bore alignment determines sealing performance — single-setup 5-axis machining eliminates the positional error accumulation of multi-setup 3-axis approaches.

Surface finishing for energy components is primarily passivation. Passivation per ASTM A967 (citric acid or nitric acid) is standard for 316L, duplex, and super-duplex stainless pump and valve components — the electrochemical process removes free iron from the surface and strengthens the passive oxide layer that is the primary corrosion barrier in service. Electropolishing is available for the highest-cleanliness requirements and where surface smoothness reduces fluid-borne corrosion initiation. For Inconel components, solution annealing and pickling are handling procedures that may be required at incoming material stage; confirm process requirements at quoting. Finish process records — passivation method, bath chemistry, and exposure duration — ship with every post-processed energy order.

## Section 8: 5-Axis Capability
**Design:** 2-column — text left, 5-axis machined component image right. White background.
**Heading:** Complex Geometry. One Setup.

Energy industry pump and valve components present a multi-face machining challenge where the dimensional relationships between bored features, sealing faces, and flanged connections directly determine equipment performance and pressure integrity. A choke valve body has a bore axis, a seat face, actuator mount geometry, and flange connection faces — all of which must relate to each other within dimensional limits specified by the pressure-rated design. A centrifugal pump casing has impeller bore, volute geometry, suction and discharge flanges, and seal carrier features that must all reference a common geometric datum.

5-axis machining holds all features from a single fixturing — no datum transfer, no positional error accumulation between setups. For a valve body where seat-to-bore coaxiality determines sealing performance, and for a pump casing where impeller clearance determines hydraulic efficiency, the single-datum approach is the manufacturing methodology that reliably delivers the dimensional conformance the pressure-rated design requires.

At Weldo CNC, simultaneous 5-axis machining is used for complex fluid-path geometry in pump impellers and the complex external profiles of custom manifold bodies. 3+2 positioning handles multi-face valve body geometry — flanges, port bores, and actuator interfaces on different faces — where a rigid locked setup provides the stiffest cutting conditions for heavy material removal in duplex and Inconel.

## Section 9: Documentation
**Design:** 4-column icon+description grid. White background.
**Heading:** Documentation Standard for Energy Orders

**Material Certificate**
Mill cert coverage for every energy order: alloy grade, heat number, chemical composition (including molybdenum, nitrogen, nickel, and chromium for duplex and Inconel), mechanical properties, and specification reference. For duplex 2205: ASTM A276 or EN 10088 including PREN confirmation. For super-duplex 2507: ASTM A276 super-duplex grade. For Inconel 625: AMS 5666 or equivalent; for Inconel 718: AMS 5663. For API drill string steel: API material grade certification with mechanical properties. NACE MR0175 compliance confirmation for sour service components can be provided where the material specification satisfies the standard; confirm at quoting. Heat number and specification logged before first cut.

**CMM Dimensional Report**
Feature-by-feature dimensional report: nominal, tolerance, actual, pass/fail per feature. GD&T callouts reported per drawing revision: bore concentricity and diameter; seat-to-bore coaxiality; flange face flatness; port true position; sealing face finish. For pressure-rated energy components, the CMM report is the primary dimensional conformance record — it documents that the part as machined meets the dimensional limits of the pressure-rated design.

**Finish Process Record**
For passivation (ASTM A967), electropolishing, or pickling: process type, bath chemistry, verification date, exposure duration, and post-process visual and dimensional check. For passivated duplex and super-duplex components, the process record confirms the method (citric acid, nitric acid, or electrochemical) and bath chemistry parameters. For components with functional sealing face Ra requirements, measured Ra value is documented.

**First Article Inspection (FAI)**
For new part numbers, revised drawings, or energy programme qualification: ballooned drawing with all dimensions numbered and cross-referenced to the CMM report + material cert + finish record. For energy supply chain qualification, FAI packages are structured to support vendor data submission requirements in engineering document management systems. Confirm FAI format and submission requirements at quoting.

## Section 10: Quality & Certifications
**Design:** RS-01. Dark navy background. White text.

**Pre-Production:**
Material verified against mill cert before machining begins — alloy grade, heat number, chemical composition confirmed, PREN verified for duplex and super-duplex. Drawing reviewed for GD&T callouts, critical feature identification (bore diameters, sealing faces, pressure-rated thread geometry), surface finish requirements, post-process plan, and NACE compliance documentation requirements. **In-Production:**
In-process CMM measurement at defined intervals — bore diameters measured before final passes to confirm dimensional target. Datum references verified after each setup change. **Final-Production:**
CMM inspection against current drawing revision — every critical dimension reported per feature: nominal, tolerance, actual, pass/fail. Material certificate, dimensional report, and finish process record assembled before packing. Nothing dispatched without sign-off. Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification.
