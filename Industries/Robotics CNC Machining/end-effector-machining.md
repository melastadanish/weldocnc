## Section 3: What Does CNC End Effector Machining Require?

**H2:** What Does CNC End Effector Machining Require?

Weldo CNC machines end effectors — gripper bodies, tool-changer flanges, sensor brackets, and suction cup mounts — in aluminium 6061-T6/T651, aluminium 7075-T6, PEEK, 303 stainless steel, and Ti-6Al-4V (TC4), to ISO 9409-1 flange bore tolerances of ±0.005 mm, from its ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China. Prototype end effectors ship in 1–5 business days. Every precision flange order includes a full CMM — coordinate measuring machine — dimensional inspection report covering the flange bore diameter, flange face flatness, and mounting hole bolt circle position.

End effector machining is controlled by one critical datum relationship: the ISO 9409-1 centre bore. Every feature on an end effector — the mounting hole bolt circle, gripper jaw bores, cable routing holes, sensor attachment points — must be positioned relative to that centre bore, not to an outer housing face. A bolt circle referenced to the centre bore ensures the end effector seats and registers correctly on the robot wrist every time. One measured from a housing OD accumulates the error of the housing turning and the bore, simultaneously.

Weight management is the second discipline. Every gram added to an end effector reduces robot payload capacity and increases wrist inertia — which degrades cycle time and positioning accuracy at speed. Material selection against the mass budget starts at the quote stage: 6061-T6 at 2.70 g/cm³ for standard grippers, 7075-T6 at 2.81 g/cm³ when jaw loads demand higher strength, TC4 at 4.43 g/cm³ where corrosion resistance rules out 7075, and PEEK at 1.31 g/cm³ where chemical or food-contact environments require a non-metallic body.

PEEK billets thicker than 20 mm are annealed at 200°C for 4 hours before machining to relieve internal stress and prevent dimensional shift during cutting.

## Section 6: End Effector Machining Process

**H2:** How Weldo CNC Machines End Effectors

Datum selection determines every subsequent operation. The ISO 9409-1 centre bore is established first — rough bored, then finish bored to ±0.005 mm diameter in a single clamping. All other features — the bolt circle, gripper jaw bores, cable routing holes — are then machined with the centre bore as the live datum. Repositioning the part before those features resets the datum reference and introduces cumulative position error that cannot be removed in inspection.

Flange face machining follows the bore. A face mill brings the seating surface to flatness of 0.02 mm/100 mm, with Ra 0.8 µm surface finish achieved by a finish pass with controlled feed and depth of cut. The parallelism between the flange face and the centre bore axis is a direct output of how accurately the fixture held the datum through the face milling operation — measured numerically in CMM.

Gripper jaw bores are machined last in the sequence. The jaw bore centreline position relative to the flange centre bore is a functional dimension: it determines jaw symmetry about the tool centreline. At ±0.010 mm bore diameter, the clearance fit for a linear guide or jaw rail carries no more than 0.01 mm of play in any direction, which keeps jaw tracking parallel under grip load. Thread tapping for M-series mounting bolts follows bore operations — 6H thread class on internal threads ensures consistent mating with standard ISO metric fasteners.

## Section 8: Why Engineers Specify Weldo CNC for End Effector Machining

**H2:** Why Engineers Specify Weldo CNC for End Effector Machining

**1. ISO 9409-1 flange datum — all features referenced to robot wrist interface**
The centre bore is established first, to ±0.005 mm, before any other feature is cut. Bolt circle, jaw bores, and routing holes are all machined with that bore as the live datum. This is the correct engineering sequence for an ISO 9409-1 compliant interface — not machining bolt holes from the OD and then boring the centre.

**2. Weight management integrated from material selection**
Material grade selection addresses the mass budget before machining starts: 6061-T6 for standard payloads, 7075-T6 where jaw forces require higher tensile strength, TC4 where corrosion rules out aluminium, and PEEK where non-metallic construction is mandatory. Each grade decision changes the end effector's contribution to robot wrist inertia — Weldo CNC quotes include material grade with density at the grade level, not just alloy family.

**3. PEEK end effectors for food, medical, and chemical environments**
PEEK (unfilled) end effectors are machined using sharp carbide tooling with low cutting temperatures and no coolant, which prevents thermal distortion during cutting. Thick PEEK billets are annealed at 200°C for 4 hours before first cut to relieve internal stress. The result: dimensionally stable PEEK end effectors for food-contact, sterilisation, and chemical wash environments.

**4. TC4 and 7075 machining protocols, not generic aluminium/titanium parameters**
Aluminium 7075-T6 at bore edges requires chamfer radii held as-specified — not undercut, not oversized — to prevent stress corrosion initiation at the highest-stress point in the part. TC4 requires consistent feed and speed through every bore pass; interrupted cuts accelerate work-hardening and tool wear. Both protocols are documented, not assumed.

**5. CMM bolt circle and flange face verification in the inspection report**
The CMM report covers flange bore diameter, flange face flatness, and bolt circle position — all three measurements, numerically, against drawing specification. Pass/fail is recorded per feature. The report ships with the parts.

## Section 9: FAQ

**H2:** End Effector Machining — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerance can Weldo CNC hold on an ISO 9409-1 flange bore?**

The flange bore diameter holds to ±0.005 mm — the interference fit tolerance required for correct tool-changer engagement. Mounting hole bolt circle position holds to ±0.025 mm relative to the centre bore datum. Gripper jaw bore diameter holds to ±0.010 mm for linear guide or jaw rail clearance fits. All three measurements are verified by CMM and reported numerically in the inspection report that ships with every precision end effector order.

**Q2: What materials do you machine for end effectors?**

Confirmed end effector materials include aluminium 6061-T6 and T651, aluminium 7075-T6, PEEK (unfilled), 303 stainless steel, and Ti-6Al-4V (TC4). Aluminium 6061-T651 is the standard for prototype gripper bodies — T651 provides better stress relief in thick billets than T6. PEEK is specified for food, medical, and chemical-contact end effectors. TC4 is used where 7075-T6 cannot be used due to corrosion risk. Confirm the grade and environment at the quote stage.

**Q5: What file formats do you accept for end effector quotes?**

STEP (.stp / .step) is required for 3D geometry. **Q6: How much does end effector machining cost?**

Cost drivers include material grade (TC4 and PEEK cost more per unit than 6061-T6 in billet form), feature count and cycle time (a six-jaw gripper body with six bore operations costs more than a two-jaw bracket), and inspection requirements (CMM bolt circle and flatness verification adds time). **Q7: How does Weldo CNC compare to a general CNC supplier for end effector machining?**

A general CNC supplier machines features in sequence without a fixed datum strategy. For end effectors, that means bolt circle position may be referenced to the housing OD rather than the ISO 9409-1 centre bore — and position error at the flange face accumulates into misalignment at the robot wrist. Weldo CNC establishes the centre bore first, to ±0.005 mm, and references all subsequent features from that datum. The CMM report confirms bolt circle position relative to the bore axis — not relative to an OD — which is what the ISO 9409-1 standard requires and what the tool changer interface depends on.

**Q8: How is flange face flatness verified?**

Flange face flatness is verified by CMM surface scan across the full seating face, not a single-point or three-point check. The scan generates a point cloud that reports maximum deviation and flatness across any 100 mm span. A pass/fail result against the 0.02 mm/100 mm specification is included in the inspection report. Single-point checks cannot detect localised high spots at the bore edge or face roll near the mounting hole pattern — both of which cause seating errors under bolt clamp load.

**Q9: Can you machine PEEK end effectors for food or medical environments?**

PEEK (unfilled) end effectors are confirmed. PEEK is chemically inert, tolerates sterilisation temperatures, and requires no metallic inserts for standard gripper geometries. Machining protocol: sharp carbide tooling, low cutting speeds to control heat, no coolant (coolant introduces thermal distortion risk in PEEK). Billets over 20 mm thick are annealed at 200°C for 4 hours before the first cut to relieve internal stress. Confirm the operating environment — temperature range, chemical exposure, and sterilisation method — at the quote stage.

**Q10: What is the thread class specification for end effector mounting bolts?**

Internal threads on M-series mounting bolts use 6H thread class — the ISO metric standard for precision clearance fits with standard 6g bolts. 6H ensures consistent engagement under repeated tool-changer cycles without binding. Specify thread class on your 2D drawing. If your robot wrist uses a non-standard fastener pattern, confirm the thread form, pitch, and class at the quote stage.
