## Section 3 — What Is Delrin CNC Machining?

Delrin CNC machining is the precision subtractive manufacturing of Delrin (polyoxymethylene / POM acetal) — a semi-crystalline engineering thermoplastic known for low friction, high stiffness, and excellent dimensional stability — using CNC milling, turning, and 5-axis machining centres. At Weldo CNC, located in Dongguan, Guangdong, China, Delrin parts are machined to tolerances of ±0.005 mm on 3-axis and 4-axis platforms and ±0.003 mm on 5-axis centres, with Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM) inspection reports supplied with every tight-tolerance order. Delrin's machinability is among the best of all engineering plastics — it chips cleanly, holds tight tolerances, and requires less force to cut than PEEK or nylon — making it the default specification when a low-friction, precision mechanical plastic component is required. Weldo CNC holds ISO 9001:2015 certification, with prototype delivery in 1–5 business days and small-batch production in 5–15 business days.

Engineers specify Delrin when they need a plastic that behaves predictably under the cutter, holds dimensional tolerances comparable to aluminium, and provides a self-lubricating, low-friction surface without additional coatings. Delrin's coefficient of friction (0.1–0.3 against steel), yield strength (~65 MPa), and hardness (Rockwell M80) make it suitable for load-bearing bushings, precision gears, valve seats, and sliding mechanical assemblies where metals would corrode or require lubrication.

Delrin is one of the easier plastics to machine but has specific characteristics that affect precision work: it does not absorb moisture (unlike nylon), so pre-machining conditioning is not required. However, internal stress in extruded rod stock can cause warping on thin sections when material is removed asymmetrically. Delrin also has a relatively low melting point (~175 °C) — dull tools or excessive spindle speed without adequate chip clearance generate heat that re-welds chips to the surface, causing poor finish and dimensional inaccuracy. Sharp, polished tooling and positive chip clearance are essential.

Weldo CNC has machined Delrin for food processing machinery, industrial automation, medical device production, and fluid handling equipment since 2012. These are programmes where gear pitch error, bushing bore diameter, and cam surface finish have direct impact on equipment performance and service life.

## Section 4 — Delrin Grades

Weldo CNC machines standard Delrin (acetal homopolymer and copolymer), food-grade acetal, and glass-filled POM. Grade selection is driven by the application's friction, load, temperature, and food-contact requirements.

| Grade / Variant | Key Difference | Max Continuous Temp | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Delrin 150 (acetal homopolymer) | Highest stiffness, tightest tolerances, opaque white/black | 90–100 °C | Precision gears, bearings, valve bodies, dimensionally critical mechanical parts |
| Delrin 500 (acetal homopolymer) | Higher melt flow — better for complex geometry | 90–100 °C | Complex machined features, thin walls, intricate forms |
| Acetal copolymer (POM-C) | Better chemical resistance, more stable in hot/humid environments, lower mechanical properties than homopolymer | 90–100 °C | Chemical fluid handling, food processing, wet environments |
| Food-grade acetal | FDA 21 CFR 177.2470 compliant, natural/white colour, no additives | 90 °C | Food contact components, beverage equipment, pharmaceutical machinery |
| Glass-filled POM (GF20/GF30) | Higher stiffness and wear resistance, abrasive on tools, reduced elongation | 100 °C | High-load bearings, structural components, conveyor parts |

> **Food-grade note:** Food-grade acetal satisfies FDA 21 CFR 177.2470 for food contact. Standard Delrin with colorant additives or internal lubricants may not be FDA-compliant — specify "food-grade" or "natural acetal" when food or pharmaceutical contact is required.

## Section 5 — Delrin or Alternative Material — Which Fits Your Part?

**Choose Delrin When:**
- A self-lubricating, low-friction mechanical component is needed (bushings, gears, cams, slides)
- Dimensional stability is required at moderate temperatures (up to 90–100 °C)
- Food contact or pharmaceutical contact compliance is required (food-grade acetal)
- Machinability and cost efficiency are priorities — Delrin is one of the most economical engineering plastics to machine
- Example parts: gear blanks, conveyor rollers, valve balls and seats, instrument knobs, cable-routing bushings

**When an Alternative May Win:**
- Operating temperature exceeds 100 °C → **PEEK** is required (handles up to 260 °C continuous); Delrin softens above 90–100 °C
- Aggressive chemical exposure — concentrated acids, hydrocarbons → **PEEK** or **PTFE** (Delrin has moderate chemical resistance; strong acids attack it)
- Maximum impact toughness → **Nylon PA6/PA66** (tougher under impact, though less dimensionally stable than Delrin)
- Optical clarity → **Polycarbonate (PC)** or **Acrylic (PMMA)** — Delrin is opaque
- Medical implant or sterilisation compatibility → **PEEK** (Delrin is not autoclavable at high cycle counts and is not suitable for implants)

*If the application sits on the boundary between Delrin and PEEK or Delrin and nylon, send the STEP file with operating temperature, chemical exposure, and load conditions — the right material will be confirmed before quoting.*

**Thermal expansion note:** Delrin has a coefficient of thermal expansion (CTE) of approximately 110–120 × 10⁻⁶/°C — roughly 5–6× higher than steel and 3–4× higher than aluminium. ## Section 10 — Delrin Machining Design Guidelines

| Feature | Recommendation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Wall thickness | Minimum 1.5 mm for free-standing walls; 2.0 mm+ for load-bearing walls | Delrin is stiffer than nylon but thin walls still deflect under cutting forces and may warp as internal stress releases |
| Internal corners | Minimum radius 0.5 mm; match end mill radius or add 10% clearance | Zero-radius internal corners require EDM; forced milling causes tool vibration in Delrin |
| Tooling | Sharp, polished, single-flute or two-flute carbide — positive rake angle | Dull tools generate heat that re-welds chips to the Delrin surface, destroying finish and tolerance |
| Coolant | Air blast preferred; avoid flood coolant where possible — Delrin is not moisture-sensitive but flood coolant can introduce thermal gradient | Chip heat must clear; air blast is sufficient for most Delrin operations |
| Chip clearance | High chip-load strategies and frequent pecking on deep pockets — Delrin chips are short but can pack in deep features | Packed chips re-heat the cut zone and cause dimensional drift |
| Thread design | Cut threads perform well in Delrin; for repeated high-torque assembly, use helicoil or metal thread inserts in the most critical locations | Delrin threads are strong for their load class but do not resist stripping as well as metal in high-cycle fastener locations |
| Asymmetric sections | Leave balanced machining stock when removing large volumes from one side of a billet — machine in balanced passes | Extruded Delrin rod has internal stress; asymmetric material removal releases stress and causes bowing on thin parts |
| Gear tooth geometry | Machine gear profiles with fine stepover — Ra 0.8 µm or better on tooth flanks | Delrin gear noise and wear life are directly related to tooth surface finish quality |
| Food-grade material | Specify "food-grade acetal" or "natural Delrin" explicitly — confirm no coloured or internally lubricated grades are used | Colour pigments and internal lubricants in standard Delrin may not meet FDA 21 CFR 177.2470 |
| Dimensional measurement | Measure at ambient temperature (23 °C) — allow 15–20 min stabilisation after machining | Residual heat from machining causes temporary dimensional expansion; measuring hot gives false readings |

**Most common DFM failure — warping on asymmetric thin sections:** Delrin extruded rod stock contains residual manufacturing stress. When a thin flange, plate, or unsupported wall is machined, the removal of material from one side releases stress and causes bowing. This is most common on parts with large flat features and thin cross-sections (e.g. a 3 mm flat flange turned from 50 mm rod). Weldo CNC mitigates this by roughing in balanced passes, stress-relieving at low temperature (70 °C for 2 hours) before finish machining where the geometry warrants it, and designing the fixturing to control deflection. Advise at quoting if thin, flat, or asymmetric Delrin geometry is involved.

**DFM review catches these issues before cutting:** Weldo CNC reviews Delrin drawings for minimum wall thickness, asymmetric material removal risk, thread engagement, and food-contact grade requirements. These are flagged at Design for Manufacturability (DFM) review before the job enters production — not after a warped plate is delivered.

## Section 15 — FAQ

**Q1: What tolerances can Weldo CNC hold on Delrin?**
Standard tolerances on Delrin milling are ±0.005 mm, verified by CMM (Coordinate Measuring Machine). ISO 2768-c is the baseline standard for plastics. Bore tolerances to H7 and H6 are achievable for bushing and shaft-fit applications. All tight-tolerance Delrin parts are measured after thermal stabilisation at ambient temperature — not immediately after machining when the part is still warm.

**Q2: What is Delrin CNC machining and how does it differ from machining metals?**
Delrin CNC machining is the removal of material from Delrin (POM acetal) stock using CNC milling, turning, and drilling operations — the same subtractive process as metal machining. Key differences: Delrin requires less cutting force than metals, but demands sharp polished tooling to prevent heat-welding chips back to the surface. Feeds and speeds are adjusted to manage thermal input — Delrin has a low melting point (175 °C) and dull tools or fast spindle speeds cause surface smearing. Flood coolant is not always needed; air blast typically provides adequate chip and heat clearance.

**Q3: What Delrin grades are available?**
Weldo CNC machines Delrin 150 (acetal homopolymer — highest stiffness, tightest tolerances), Delrin 500 (homopolymer, higher flow for complex features), acetal copolymer (POM-C — better chemical resistance, marginally lower mechanical properties), food-grade acetal (FDA 21 CFR 177.2470 compliant, natural/white), and glass-filled POM (GF20/GF30 — higher stiffness and wear resistance). Specify grade on the drawing; "Delrin" without grade designation defaults to Delrin 150 homopolymer.

**Q4: What are the lead times for Delrin parts?**
Prototype delivery is 1–5 business days from drawing approval. Small-batch production is 5–15 business days. Larger production runs are confirmed at quoting. Quote turnaround is 24 hours from STEP file and drawing submission. No pre-machining conditioning is required for Delrin — it can go straight from stock to machining without drying time.

**Q5: Is there a minimum order quantity for Delrin machining?**
No minimum order quantity. Single prototype Delrin parts are accepted with the same quality standards and CMM inspection as production runs. Delrin's machinability means single-part setups are economical compared to metals — there is no premium for small quantities beyond standard per-part pricing.

**Q6: What file formats does Weldo CNC accept for Delrin parts?**
STEP (.stp, .step) is the preferred format for 3D geometry. IGES (.igs), Parasolid (.x_t), and SolidWorks (.sldprt) files are also accepted. 2D drawings should be supplied as PDF to confirm tolerances, surface finish callouts, thread specifications, and material grade — especially for food-contact programmes where grade compliance must be confirmed before quoting.

**Q7: What drives the cost of Delrin CNC machining?**
Delrin raw material is inexpensive relative to engineering metals and high-performance plastics such as PEEK — material cost is a smaller fraction of part cost than for titanium or stainless steel. Machining cost is driven primarily by part complexity, number of setups, and feature density. Food-grade acetal has a modest material premium over standard Delrin. Tight tolerances requiring full CMM inspection add inspection time. Gear profiles and precision bores with thread features are the most common sources of machining complexity on Delrin parts.

**Q8: When should I choose Delrin over PEEK or nylon?**
Delrin is the correct choice when the operating temperature is below 90–100 °C, chemical exposure is moderate (no strong acids), and a self-lubricating, dimensionally stable mechanical component is needed at reasonable cost. PEEK is required when temperature exceeds 100 °C or aggressive chemical resistance is needed. Nylon (PA6/PA66) is preferred when impact toughness is the primary driver; it is tougher than Delrin under shock but absorbs moisture and has less dimensional stability. Delrin is typically 60–80% less expensive to machine per part than PEEK and offers superior dimensional stability compared to nylon.

**Q9: Is food-grade Delrin available, and what compliance documentation is supplied?**
Yes. Food-grade acetal (natural white or black, without pigment additives or internal lubricants) is available and satisfies FDA 21 CFR 177.2470 for food contact. Weldo CNC supplies a material certificate referencing FDA 21 CFR 177.2470 with every food-contact Delrin order. For European programmes, EN 10204 3.1 material certification is available. Specify "food-grade" or "FDA-compliant acetal" on the drawing and at quoting to ensure the correct material grade is sourced.

**Q10: How does Delrin perform in high-cycle gear and bushing applications?**
Delrin is one of the best self-lubricating plastics for gear and bushing service: low coefficient of friction (0.1–0.3 against steel without external lubricant), good fatigue strength for a thermoplastic, and predictable wear rate. Performance is strongly related to tooth surface finish — Ra 0.8 µm or better on tooth flanks reduces initial wear-in and improves noise. For demanding applications (high load, elevated temperature, aggressive media), PEEK or PTFE-filled grades should be evaluated. Delrin is not suitable above 100 °C continuous service — if the gear or bushing runs hot, specify PEEK.
