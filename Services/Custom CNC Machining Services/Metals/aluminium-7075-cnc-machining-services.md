## Section 3: What is Aluminium 7075 CNC Machining?

**H2:** What is Aluminium 7075 CNC Machining?

Weldo CNC machines aluminium 7075-T6 — the highest-strength common aluminium alloy — to tolerances of ±0.005 mm across milling, turning, and 5-axis operations, from its ISO 9001:2015 certified facility in Dongguan, China. Prototype parts ship in 1–5 business days. Every tight-tolerance order includes a full CMM — coordinate measuring machine — dimensional inspection report, delivered with the parts.

7075-T6 is specified when the design load exceeds what 6061-T6 can safely carry at the required part mass. Tensile strength of 572 MPa and yield strength of 503 MPa — nearly double 6061-T6 — allow engineers to design thinner walls and lighter sections without reducing safety margins. The zinc-copper alloy chemistry produces this strength through precipitation hardening, but at a trade-off: 7075 work-hardens more readily than 6061 under aggressive cuts, and its anodizing is less predictable.

Machinability of 7075-T6 is good — chips form cleanly at high cutting speed. But feed rate and depth of cut must be managed more carefully than on 6061. The tool nose radius and cutting edge sharpness are more critical — a marginally worn insert on 7075 that would still produce acceptable finish on 6061 produces surface tearing and built-up edge on 7075. Cutting parameters are grade-specific, not shared with 6061 programmes.

Weldo CNC has machined 7075-T6 components since 2012. Typical part types include aerospace structural brackets, UAV and drone airframes, defence fittings, high-load robotic arm structures, and motorsport suspension components.

## Section 5: Material Trade-off Cards

**H2:** When 7075-T6 is Right — and When 6061 or 2024 is Better

**Subheading:** 7075-T6's strength advantage is real — but it comes with trade-offs that affect cost, finish, and post-process options.

**Card 1 — When 7075-T6 is the Right Choice**

The load case demands it. UAV frame members that must survive crash loads at minimum mass, aerospace brackets carrying fatigue-critical structural loads, and defence fittings where the safety factor collapses without 7075's strength — these are the correct applications. At 572 MPa tensile, 7075-T6 delivers structural performance approaching some steels at one-third the density. Nothing else in the common aluminium range comes close.

*Key spec: Tensile 572 MPa · Yield 503 MPa · Density 2.81 g/cm³ · Strongest common aluminium alloy*

**Card 2 — When 6061-T6 is Sufficient**

If the load case is satisfied by 310 MPa tensile strength, 6061-T6 is the better choice. It machines faster (higher cutting speeds), anodizes more evenly, welds more cleanly, and costs less per kilogram. 7075 is not "better than 6061 in all ways" — it is stronger, but specifically worse at anodizing uniformity, weldability, and corrosion resistance in un-anodized condition. Choose 7075 only when the load or fatigue requirement actually requires it.

*Key spec: 6061-T6 tensile 310 MPa — sufficient for 80% of aluminium machining applications*

**Card 3 — When SCC Risk Demands 7075-T73**

7075-T6 is susceptible to stress-corrosion cracking (SCC) — failure under sustained tensile stress in a humid or corrosive environment. Aerospace and marine structures that cannot be inspected frequently, or parts loaded in tension and exposed to salt spray, should specify 7075-T73 or T7351. The overaged temper sacrifices 70 MPa of tensile strength but dramatically reduces SCC susceptibility. Confirm the operating environment with your structural engineer before specifying T6 in humid service.

*Key spec: 7075-T73 tensile 503 MPa — SCC resistance improved over T6 at modest strength cost*

## Section 10: Aluminium 7075 CNC Machining Design Guidelines

**H2:** Aluminium 7075 Design Guidelines

**Subheading:** 7075-T6 imposes additional DFM constraints compared to 6061 — strength comes with specific machining and finishing trade-offs.

| Feature | Recommendation | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Anodize specification | Use alodine (chromate conversion) for corrosion protection unless cosmetic anodize is functionally required | 7075 anodize is thinner and less uniform than 6061 — colour consistency is harder to achieve. Alodine provides corrosion protection without dimensional growth |
| Wall thickness | Minimum 0.8 mm — same as 6061, but with lower deflection resistance at the same thickness | 7075 is denser and stiffer, but thin-wall deflection under end-mill cutting force is similar to 6061 — don't reduce wall thickness because the grade is stronger |
| Cutting speed | Reduce cutting speed 10–15% versus 6061 programme on same geometry | 7075-T6 work-hardens faster under excessive heat — keeping cutting speed controlled prevents surface quality degradation |
| Tool sharpness | Use fresh inserts when finishing critical surfaces on 7075 | A worn insert produces tearing and built-up edge on 7075 at conditions that still give acceptable finish on 6061 |
| Thread engagement | Minimum 2× diameter for M8 and below | 7075 is stronger than 6061 but thread strip force is still lower than steel — don't apply steel thread-depth logic to 7075 |
| SCC-risk environments | Specify 7075-T73 or T7351 for parts in sustained tensile stress and humid/salt environments | T6 is susceptible to stress-corrosion cracking under these conditions — T73 dramatically reduces the risk |
| Flatness of large blocks | Specify T651 plate for blocks over 30 mm thick | Residual rolling stress in T6 plate releases during machining and warps large blocks — T651 eliminates most of this risk |
| Post-weld strength | Do not rely on 7075 weld joints for structural loads | 7075 is not readily weldable — heat-affected zone loses most of the T6 precipitation-hardened strength. Design bolted or press-fit joints for 7075 assemblies |

**Most common DFM failures on aluminium 7075 parts:**

Anodize failures are the most common 7075-specific issue. Engineers specify Type II anodize in the same colour range as 6061 parts and discover the oxide layer is thinner and the colour is inconsistent — 7075's zinc and copper alloying elements suppress the anodizing uniformity. The fix is either to use alodine (which provides corrosion protection without cosmetic anodize demands) or to test anodize colour on a sample piece before running production.

Residual stress warping in large 7075-T6 blocks is the second pattern. A 200 × 150 × 80 mm block loses 0.1–0.3 mm of flatness across its largest face as machining removes material from both sides. Specifying T651 stress-relieved plate from the start eliminates most of this — Weldo CNC flags T6 vs T651 at DFM review for blocks above the threshold size.

## Section 15: FAQ

**H2:** Aluminium 7075 CNC Machining — Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What tolerance can Weldo CNC hold on aluminium 7075 parts?**

Aluminium 7075-T6 machines to ±0.005 mm on precision features — position of holes, bore diameters, OD fits — verified by CMM. General dimensions follow ISO 2768-m at ±0.05 mm. Surface finish on milled 7075-T6 is Ra 1.6 µm on standard finish passes; Ra 0.8 µm with an additional finishing pass. Specify critical features and tolerances on your drawing.

**Q2: Is aluminium 7075 harder to machine than 6061?**

7075-T6 is slightly more demanding than 6061-T6 but still has good machinability. Cutting speeds are reduced 10–15% to prevent work hardening on the surface layer. Tool sharpness requirements are tighter — a marginally worn insert that would still give acceptable finish on 6061 produces tearing on 7075. Clean chip formation at correct cutting parameters is achievable with grade-specific tooling strategy. Overall cycle time on identical geometry is 15–25% longer on 7075 versus 6061.

**Q3: What 7075 tempers do you machine?**

Confirmed tempers include 7075-T6, T651, T73, and T7351. T6 is the standard high-strength specification. T651 is T6 stress-relieved by stretching — recommended for precision blocks and large plates where residual stress would cause warping. T73 and T7351 are overaged for stress-corrosion cracking resistance — specified for parts in sustained tensile stress and humid or salt-spray environments.

**Q4: How long does it take to machine aluminium 7075 parts?**

Prototype 7075 parts ship in 1–5 business days from drawing approval. Small-batch production ships in 5–15 business days. 7075 cycle time is 15–25% longer than 6061 on identical geometry — this is reflected in per-part pricing at prototype quantities. Upload your STEP file for a 24-hour quote.

**Q5: What is the minimum order quantity for aluminium 7075 CNC machining?**

No minimum order quantity. Single-piece prototypes accepted. The quote process is identical whether you order 1 part or 1,000.

**Q6: What file formats do you accept for aluminium 7075 quotes?**

STEP (.stp / .step) is the preferred 3D format. Include a 2D PDF drawing with tolerance callouts, surface finish specifications, temper designation, thread class, and anodize or alodine specification. For large blocks and plates, note T651 requirement if flatness is critical.

**Q7: What drives the cost of aluminium 7075 CNC machining?**

7075 bar and plate cost more per kilogram than 6061. Cycle time is 15–25% longer. Insert replacement rate is higher. These three factors make 7075 typically 20–40% more expensive per part than identical geometry in 6061. When the load case requires 7075, that cost is an engineering necessity — but confirm the load requirement before specifying 7075, because 6061 at the same tolerance delivers the part more economically where strength permits.

**Q8: How does Weldo CNC compare to other aluminium 7075 machining suppliers?**

Grade-specific cutting parameters and DFM support are the measurable differences. 7075 machined at 6061 parameters produces work-hardened surface layers and inconsistent finish — Weldo CNC applies grade-specific settings. Anodize specification and SCC temper selection are reviewed at quote stage, not discovered after delivery. ISO 9001:2015 with a 97.48% annual pass rate and CMM reports in every tight-tolerance shipment provide the documented quality assurance that aerospace and defence customers require.

**Q9: Can aluminium 7075 be anodized?**

Yes, but with important constraints. 7075 anodizes to a thinner and less uniform oxide layer than 6061 due to its zinc and copper content. Type II anodize is achievable, but colour uniformity is lower than 6061 — cosmetic colour matching across assemblies mixing 6061 and 7075 will show visual differences. Type III hard anodize is technically possible but less commonly specified on 7075 due to the same uniformity concerns. Alodine (chromate conversion coating) is often the better choice for 7075 — it provides corrosion protection without the colour uniformity limitation.

**Q10: What is the maximum size of aluminium 7075 part Weldo CNC can machine?**

CNC milling handles up to 4,000 × 1,500 × 600 mm. CNC turning handles up to Ø200 mm × 500 mm. For large structural 7075 parts — over 300 mm in any dimension from precision block stock — specify T651 temper. Contact the quoting team on parts approaching the upper size limits — fixturing and residual stress management are discussed at quote stage.
